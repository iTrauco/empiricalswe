          <p>Axles are the simplest form of mechanical energy transfer. They come in a few varieties:</p>

<ul>
<li>Wood (t1): 2 resistance, 2 bearing</li>
<li>Stone (t1): 0 resistance, 0 bearing</li>
<li>Flint<code>*</code> (t1): N/A resistance, N/A bearing</li>
<li>Redstone<code>**</code> (t1): N/A resistance, N/A bearing</li>
<li>Quartz<code>***</code> (t1): N/A resistance, N/A bearing</li>
<li>Iron (t2): 2 resistance, 10 bearing</li>
<li>Diamond (t2): 3 resistance, 20 bearing</li>
<li>Bedrock<code>****</code> (N/A): 0 resistance, âˆž bearing</li>
</ul>
<p>You may not have more than one type of axle in a line. The exceptions are Flint, Redstone and Quartz, of which you can have one of each in any line. However, they must be bound to another axle to turn.</p>
<p><code>*</code> Flint axles will start a fire below them when connected to an iron axle, and turning with at least 2 torque.<br/>
<code>**</code> Redstone axles will emit a signal based on how much speed they are currently rotating with, out of 20. A comparator output will give the torque, out of 20.<br/>
<code>***</code> Quartz axles will light up as they turn, a piezoelectric property. The light level is equal to the torque; 15 torque is maximum light, and 0 is no light.<br/>
<code>****</code> Bedrock axles can only be obtained in creative mode, and have no crafting recipe.</p>

<p>Axles have two stats: Resistance, and load bearing.<br/>
Resistance is how much torque the axle will take off when it turns. The lower the resistance, the more efficient the axle.<br/>
Bearing is how much torque the axle can take. If you try to run more than that through the axle, it will shred itself to pieces, dropping the materials for the axle.<br/>
You can only have axles that are made of the same material all the way through. Otherwise, the axle won't turn.</p>

<p>Axles are crafted simply.<br/>
<code>B B</code><br/>
<code>MMM</code><br/>
<code>B B</code><br/>
B is the binding. It's sticks for t1 axles, and Kenaf Rope for t2 axles. M is the material for the axle.</p>
