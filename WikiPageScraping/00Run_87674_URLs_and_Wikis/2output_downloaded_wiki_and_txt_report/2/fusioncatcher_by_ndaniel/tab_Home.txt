<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#description" id="user-content-description"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Description</h2>
<p>FusionCatcher searches for novel/known somatic fusion genes, translocations, and
chimeras in RNA-seq data (paired-end or single-end reads from Illumina NGS platforms
like Solexa/HiSeq/NextSeq/MiSeq) from diseased samples.</p>
<p>The aims of FusionCatcher are:</p>
<ul>
<li>very good detection rate for finding candidate somatic fusion
genes (see somatic mutations; using a matched normal sample is
optional; several databases of known fusion genes found in healthy
samples are used as a list of known false positives; biological
knowledge is used, like for example gene fusion between a gene and
its pseudogene is filtered out),</li>
<li>very good RT-PCR validation rate of found candidate somatic fusion
genes (this is very important for us),</li>
<li>very easy to use (i.e. no a priori knowledge of bioinformatic
databases and bioinformatics is needed in order to run FusionCatcher BUT
Linux/Unix knowledge is needed; it allows a very high level of control
for expert users),</li>
<li>to be as automatic as possible (i.e. the FusionCatcher will choose
automatically the best parameters in order to find candidate somatic
fusion genes, e.g. finding automatically the adapters, quality trimming
of reads, building the exon-exon junctions automatically based on the
length of the reads given as input, etc. while giving also full control
to expert users) while providing the best possible detection rate for
finding somatic fusion genes (with a very low rate of false positives
but a very good precision).</li>
</ul>
