<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#did-you-know" id="user-content-did-you-know"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Did You Know?:</h2>
<p>The emulators with graphic icons in the list are the "recommended" emulator for that particular system.  The emulators with plain text in the list are alternate emulators for that each particular system, and may work better for some games than others... try them out!  (For instance, <code>Final Fantasy 3</code> has serious musical accuracy issues on the recommended emulator, but plays perfectly on the <code>Snes9xNextPi2</code> emulator!)</p>
<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#set-up-your-wifi-connection" id="user-content-set-up-your-wifi-connection"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Set up your wifi connection:</h2>
<ol>
<li>Go to the <code>Settings</code> page (left PS3 controller icon, background is 8-bit Professor Farnsworth facing LEFT)</li>
<li>Select <code>WIFI_NETWORKS</code> and press X on the controller or ENTER on the keyboard</li>
<li>Use arrow keys up / down to select your network, press RIGHT arrow to configure</li>
<li>Set up your network, press F10 to save and then capital Q to quit.  Be sure to select <code>Automatically connect to this network</code> if you dont want to have to do this every time you reboot!</li>
<li>NOTE that the wifi network takes a long time to connect during startup, and may not be connected by the time the automatic Primestation update call takes place.  To retry updating the Primestation, just press START and then choose <code>QUIT</code>, and then <code>QUIT EMULATIONSTATION</code>, and it will automatically update and restart.</li>
</ol>

<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#to-utilize-cloud-backup-and-restore-of-save-states--srams--memory-cards--etc" id="user-content-to-utilize-cloud-backup-and-restore-of-save-states--srams--memory-cards--etc"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>To utilize cloud backup and restore of save states / srams / memory cards / etc:</h2>
<ol>
<li>On a web browser, go to <a href="http://mega.co.nz" rel="nofollow">http://mega.co.nz</a> and sign up for a free cloud storage account (50GB!)</li>
<li>On the Primestation, go to the Settings page (left PS3 controller icon, background is 8-bit Professor Farnsworth facing LEFT)</li>
<li>Enter the <code>CLOUDBACKUP</code> submenu</li>
<li>Choose <code>MEGA_CLOUD_LOGIN</code>, when prompted enter the email and password you used for your mega.co.nz cloud storage account.  You should see a brief listing of standard folders such as Trash once your login is successful, although this won't be on the screen long.</li>
<li>Now, how you manage your saves is up to you:</li>
<li>
<code>MEGA_CLOUD_BACKUP_ALL_SAVESTATES_AND_SRAMS</code> will simply do as it says and back up all your current saves to the cloud drive.  Any backups currently in the cloud will be lost as the archive is completely replaced (although one backup is kept for convenience).</li>
<li>
<code>MEGA_CLOUD_RESTORE_ALL_SAVESTATES_AND_SRAMS</code> will simply do as it says and restore all your saves from the cloud drive, overwriting any conflicting game saves you have currently on the Primestation.</li>
<li>
<code>MEGA_INTELLIGENT_CLOUD_SYNC_ALL_SAVESTATES_AND_SRAMS</code> will attempt to perform an intelligent, nondestructive cloud save backup / sync operation.  First, your Primestation saves are backed up and moved out of the way locally.  Then, any existing cloud saves are restored.  Then, the local save backup we just made is restored on top of the cloud saves, for an updated combination of the two.  Then, this set of saves is backed up to the cloud and left on the Primestation (it is essentially a restore and a backup performed intelligently).</li>
<li>After you make a backup, it is a good idea to go back to the web browser and log in to your mega.co.nz account to review; you should now see a <code>PrimestationCloud</code> folder, with a <code>PrimeStationSaveArchive.tar.bz2</code> (and possibly a backup as well of the previous archive, just in case).</li>
</ol>
