<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#botania" id="user-content-botania"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Botania</h1>
<ol>
<li>Initial gathering:
<ol>
<li>4 white petals, 2 brown petals, 1 red petal, 1 light grey petal (for a pure daisy and endoflame), any 4 other (for a wand of the forest, petal apothecary and 1 mana spreader)
<ol>
<li>Each mystical flower gives 2 petals if directly crafted into petals, but 4 if bone meal is used to make a Tall Mystical Flower. Shears are necessary to harvest these.</li>
<li>If the right color flowers can't be found naturally, Floral Fertilizer is either 1 bone meal and 4 floral powder, or 1 bone meal and 2 vanilla red dye and 2 vanilla yellow dye. This produces 3-4 mystical flowers.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>At least 12 logs and 5 smooth stone. <em>Much</em> more will be needed before long.</li>
<li>One gold ingot</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Once you have a pure daisy, begin making living wood and living rock until you can produce:
<ol>
<li>Mana pool (5 living rock)</li>
<li>Mana spreader (6 living wood, gold ingot, mystical petal)</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
<a name="user-content-roots"></a>
<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#roots-2" id="user-content-roots-2"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Roots 2</h1>
<ol>
<li>Initial preparation
<ol>
<li>Find a circle
<ul>
<li>Look for shadows on the ground near pillars of vanilla cobble/mossy cobble</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Obtain books
<ol>
<li>Finding a hut saves on mats, but might not be feasible</li>
<li>Failing that, books are standard JEI recipes</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Craft mortar and pestle (standard JEI recipes)
<ol>
<li>This requires diorite</li>
<li>Not necessary if you found a hut; guaranteed to have a set</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Make herbs
<ol>
<li>Place key mat (bold item in herb list) on large node in stone circle</li>
<li>Place remainder on small nodes</li>
<li>Crops with growth stages must be fully grown
<ul>
<li>Wildroot and terra moss are the most important to start with</li>
<li>This is enough to make shatter, and wildfire, which are both fueled by wildroot.</li>
<li>Others can be added later if necessary</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Farm herbs until you have an adequate supply</li>
<li>Make infuser and staff (standard recipes in JEI)</li>
<li>Craft spells.
<ol>
<li>Place full recipe in mortar (ex. azure bluet, bonemeal, flint, terra moss, wildroot for Shatter)</li>
<li>Right click mortar with pestle to get appropriate powder</li>
<li>Right click infuser with the staff</li>
<li>Right click with powder</li>
<li>Staff now contains the supplied spell</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Prepare powder pouch(s) for fueling spells
<ol>
<li>Make powder pouch (standard recipe in JEI)</li>
<li>fill powder pouch with fuel herbs (pouch, pestle, and some number of appropriate herb in a crafting grid)
<ul>
<li>ex wildroot for Shatter</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Repeat for additional spell types as needed</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>

</ul>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#notable-roots-spells" id="user-content-notable-roots-spells"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Notable roots spells</h3>
<pre><code>Name(fuel): ingredients
   Notes
</code></pre>
<ul>
<li>Shatter(wildroot):  azure bluet, bonemeal, flint, terra moss, wildroot
<ul>
<li>Range further than reach but not extreme</li>
<li>Can mine obsidian, ardite, cobalt, possibly anything that can be mined</li>
<li>Can successfully harvest hives(!)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Wildfire(wildroot):  orange tulip, orange dye, gunpowder, charcoal, wildroot
<ul>
<li>Flamethrower-esque damage spell.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Growth Infusion(terra moss): terra moss, pereskia blossom, spruce sapling, lilac, birch sapling
<ul>
<li>Single tick of growth per use</li>
<li>Works on ender lily (although still rate limited by calendar)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Petal Shell(aubergine seed, moonglow leaf): moonglow leaf, pereskia bulb, peony, pink dye, melon seed
<ul>
<li>Adds ablative defenses that stack up to three shots; one per cast.  They seem to last indefinitely.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Sanctuary(aubergine seed, moonglow leaf): moonglow leaf, wildroot, vines, red tulip, red dye
<ul>
<li>Blocks incoming projectiles</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<a name="user-content-astralsorcery"></a>
<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#astral-sorcery" id="user-content-astral-sorcery"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Astral Sorcery</h1>
<ol>
<li>Preparation
<ul>
<li>Locate a crystal bearing shrine; the large ones with the involved roof structure will have one, as will the ones that are smothered in sand.</li>
<li>Secure 1 coal, 2 crafting tables, 1 ender pearl, 2 aquamarines, and 16 marble.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Resonating wand
<ul>
<li>Break into the container under the crystal bearing shine, and place one of the crafting tables directly underneath the crystal.  If a faint line connects the crystal to the crafting table, then things are set up right.</li>
<li>Craft the wand in that crafting table, using the pearl, aquamarines, and two marble.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Luminous crafting table
<ul>
<li>At the crystal bearing shrine, at the crafting table under it, make sooty marble using the coal and 8 marble, then make the luminous crafting table with the remaining marble, one sooty marble, and the crafting table.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>In parallel:
<ol>
<li>At night while holding the resonating wand, puffs of light will emerge from the ground in some locations.  Underneath those buffs, close to bedrock, is a single rock crystal ore block, which may drop multiple crystals.  You need at least two ore blocks to really proceed, but more is better if they are convenient to find.</li>
<li>Place the luminous crafting table down and open its GUI.  The meter at the bottom will fill partiallyâ€”during the day, it will fill hardly at all.  Experiment a bit with other locations, but you don't have the tool to find the good locations yet.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Either of these steps will improve the starlight.
<ul>
<li>Make a sky wave resonator.
<ol>
<li>Make a sky wave resonator using the crystal you got from the previous step in the luminous crafting table.  If you can't make it because there isn't enough starlight, starlight will increase as the y level increases.</li>
<li>Holding the sky wave resonator, look around at night for inky black/blue clouds near the ground.  They are somewhat uncommon, you may not have one near you.  These represent locations that will receive increased starlight.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Make spectral relays.  Spectral relays are relatively easy to make blocks that, in combination with a small multiblock structure and a plain lens, can be placed around the altar to increase the amount of starlight given.  Three was enough for me to bootstrap an altar to t3 and make a collector crystal, which is then enough to make anything in the mod.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>

<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#compact-machines" id="user-content-compact-machines"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Compact Machines</h1>
<ol>
<li>Start with a Personal Shrinking Device (2 ender pearls, iron ingot, glass pane, book).
<ul>
<li>This is both a crucial tool for interacting with the machines, it's also an ersatz book. Don't mistake this for informative.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Make a set of Miniaturization Projectors (2 diamonds, eye of ender, redstone torch, your PSD)
<ul>
<li>This doesn't consume the PSD, fortunately.</li>
<li>You get FOUR from this one crafting recipe, so you don't need to do it more than once unless you for some reason need multiple arrays.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Clear out a MASSIVE FUCK SPACE for the miniaturization array.
<ul>
<li>You can't get anything productive done in the mod without at minimum a 5x5x5 workspace, which means a 13x13x13 array.</li>
<li>Strongly consider a bigger area. The maximum available recipe size requires a 25x25x25 array (although a 29x29x29 is possible)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Assemble miniaturization array.
<ul>
<li>It has pretty strong opinions about placement of the projectors, but it also gives you graphical guides for placement when you right click a projector.</li>
<li>The projectors will complain if there's any blocks touching the outside edges of the workspace (aside from down).</li>
<li>You'll need to go up a bit for the larger arrays. While you're allowed to have a floor directly underneath the workspace, the projectors want to be centered relative the workspace faces.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Make Compact Machine Walls.
<ol>
<li>Place a 3x3 of blocks of iron on the floor of the workspace.
<ul>
<li>Positioning within the workspace doesn't actually matter as long as the blocks are aligned on the x/z plane, and there's no other blocks in the workspace</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Toss in a piece of redstone dust to start the crafting process.
<ul>
<li>You can't do more than one batch at once with a given array.</li>
<li>Automation is presumably possible, but tedious at best.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>Make tunnels (recipe in JEI).
<ul>
<li>You'll probably need a few of these per machine.</li>
<li>The recipe makes two.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Actually make some compact machines.
<ol>
<li>The recipes are all hollow cubes. The table below includes the sizes and material costs.
<ul>
<li>The two highest tiers replace the cube edges with alternate blocks; gold for Giant, diamond for Maximum.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Compact Machines are edited by using the PSD on one from the outside.</li>
<li>Once inside, you can shift-right click straight down with the PSD to set your entry point for future visits.
<ul>
<li>DO NOT BUILD OVER YOUR ENTRY POINT</li>
<li>The mod DOES NOT make any attempt to save you from this error</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Place tunnels where needed to provide exterior connections to interior blocks.
<ul>
<li>Tunnels appear to be universal connections. They transit fluid, items, and energy, and appear bidirectional.</li>
<li>You can have at most 6 tunnels in one compact machine as no two tunnels can share an external face.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Build as desired.</li>
<li>Exit the compact machine by right-clicking any wall with the PSD.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
