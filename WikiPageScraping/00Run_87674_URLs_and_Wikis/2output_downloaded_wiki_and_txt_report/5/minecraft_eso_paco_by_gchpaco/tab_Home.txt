<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#esopaco-pack-overview" id="user-content-esopaco-pack-overview"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>esopaco pack overview</h1>
<p>A modpack by esotericist and gchpaco primarily for private SMP use.</p>
<p>This is a 'high diversity' pack, intended to provide a large number of possible paths to achieving goals, in both magic and tech flavorings. There's also a very heavy emphasis on aesthetic building options (as paco is prone to strange moods claiming a workshop and demanding chiseled blocks, chiseled blocks, custom doors, wiring, open terrain).</p>
<p>The rest of this page discusses substantial deviations from vanilla minecraft that are pertinent to one not well versed in what to expect from modded minecraft.</p>
<p>Vanilla features are not mentioned, nor are things that supplement things in a mostly vanilla-ish fashion (such as the various modded biomes that are mostly different from an aesthetic standpoint).</p>

<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#overworld-generation" id="user-content-overworld-generation"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Overworld Generation</h2>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#lost-cities" id="user-content-lost-cities"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Lost Cities</h3>
<p>We rely heavily on <em>Lost Cities</em> by McJty for our primary overworld generation, using a customized profile that gets us a subset of the Biomes o' Plenty biomes, reasonably dense but spaced apart city sections, and buildings and streets that are constructed using Chisel Concrete blocks (meaning you run fast on them).</p>
<p>Each given building is likely to contain a small variety of mob spawners, and a fair number of placed loot items (including dungeon chests, potion stands, and anvils). On occasion, 'safe' versions of buildings spawn that lack spawners, but also lack loot. Don't be mistaken: just because there are no spawners, does not mean there are not mobs either spawning in the darkness, or wandering in from adjacent buildings.</p>
<p>Another notable element to Lost Cities is the subway system. The subway tunnels span the entirety of the world, even the oceans! If you need to get some where with confidence terrain won't get in your way, you can certainly find a path through these tunnels, but they are typically full of all the things that spawn in the night, and are sprinkled with the occasional subway dungeon. These dungeons have excellent rewards, but also contain spawners that aren't limited to the enemies usually found in overworld mob spawners.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#lost-souls" id="user-content-lost-souls"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Lost Souls</h3>
<p>In addition to these, the city provides a further hazard by way of <em>Lost Souls</em>, a companion mod to Lost Cities, which causes a subset of the city buildings to be 'haunted'. These haunted buildings spawn a rolling swarm of incredible spite: various vanilla enemies that ignore light levels when spawning, are extremely well geared, and have substantially increased stats over the normal versions of those enemies; it is not unusual to see endermen with over 100 health! Fortunately, once you kill enough of those enhanced enemies from a haunted building, it becomes 'cleansed', and you only have to worry about the normal encounters.</p>
<p>These buildings don't increase the amount of loot contained within, although the enemies do have a chance of dropping heavily enchanted equipment (in typically poor condition).</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#recurrent-complex-ruins-and-quark" id="user-content-recurrent-complex-ruins-and-quark"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Recurrent Complex, Ruins and Quark</h3>
<p>Between the <em>Recurrent Complex</em> mod and the <em>Ruins</em> mod, a wide variety of structures in various states of (dis)repair occur all over the world. These range from abandoned houses and barns to decaying castles, from dilapidated inns to floating towers made out of Nether materials. There are also environmental features, such as meteorite strikes filled with nether materials and lava, or large clusters of iron on the surface. These various locations can make certain materials that are hard to find or simply unavailable in the overworld (such as netherrack or soul sand, which normally are found only in the Nether) more accessible, but sometimes present their own hazards.</p>
<p><em>Quark</em>, among many many other things, adds some structures of its own, most obvious among them being the pirate ships that can be found in the oceans. While full of hostile pirates, they're also full of a moderate amount of gold, and no small quantity of fire charges and explosives.</p>
<p>There are also a few 'underground biomes' added by Quark... most are interesting aesthetically, but not important for resources. The exception is the Crystal Caves, which is a plentiful source of materials otherwise only found in dungeon chests.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#botania" id="user-content-botania"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Botania</h3>
<p>While not adding much in the terms of adventure content, <em>Botania</em> sprinkles the landscape with mystical flowers critical for building infrastructure with this tech mod in magical clothing. In an SMP context, even if you don't intend to work through its tech tree yourself, collecting the flowers can be a useful service for others that do. A floral pouch helps for this.</p>
<p>Remember: you need to use shears to break tall mystical flowers, or you don't get anything from it!</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#toroquest" id="user-content-toroquest"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>ToroQuest</h3>
<p><em>ToroQuest</em> is a mod that adds NPC factions, which are represented in every NPC village. Each has a territory that extends some distance from the village proper, where your actions can build (or cost) reputation. These factions can offer quests with a variety of rewards.</p>
<p>In addition, whenever cows spawn (or breed) there's a chance for a Toro to appear. They are, in short, asshole-grade defenders of the nearby cattle, who will wreck your day if you aren't prepared for them when you try to kill a cow.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#waystones" id="user-content-waystones"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Waystones</h3>
<p>In nearly every village a <em>Waystone</em> Shrine appears. When you activate one, you add it to your personal Waystone network, allowing you to travel between discovered waystones instantly at merely the cost of some number of experience levels depending on distance.</p>
<p>Waystones can also be constructed and placed by builders, further extending the travel network.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#forestry-and-rustic" id="user-content-forestry-and-rustic"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Forestry and Rustic</h3>
<p>While the two mods have <strong>extensive</strong> scopes beyond this, <em>Forestry</em> and <em>Rustic</em> both provide a variety of vegetation (trees) and other resources (bees, a different flavor for each mod) that can be useful to the industrious builder.</p>
<p>Remember to use a scoop for forestry hives, and you might want an apiarist backpack! Also, bees don't take kindly to either invasion of their territory or damage to their home, and will likely attack you when you encroach. The aggressiveness varies from species to species, but all can be calmed using a Smoker from Forestry. Alternately, there are protective outfits and ranged harvesting methods that can keep you safe.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#bedrock-ores" id="user-content-bedrock-ores"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Bedrock Ores</h3>
<p>In addition to the standard ore veins that show up at various depths in stone, at the bedrock level we have <em>Bedrock Ores</em> which provide ultra-high density deposits of material. While getting what you need out of these deposits require machinery and quite a bit of infrastructure to process the results, in an SMP context simply knowing where certain kinds of deposits are can be useful information even if you don't intend to take advantage of them yourself.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#ore-shrubs" id="user-content-ore-shrubs"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Ore Shrubs</h3>
<p>In dark places underground -- such as caves and the subway system -- odd plants grow known as <em>Ore Shrubs</em> that, when cultivated, slowly produce small quantities of various minerals. This makes metals (and some gems) truly renewable resources for the patient or clever builder.</p>
<p>There are also Forgotten Shrines that can spawn on the surface, which contain a Random Shrub. The berries from these bushes can be used to obtain random ores, or they can be used to craft loot bags which contain things normally found in dungeon chests, which makes them very valuable to anyone in a position to harvest from them regularly.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#roots" id="user-content-roots"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Roots</h3>
<p>The <em>Roots</em> mod is another plant-themed magic mod, with fewer infrastructure elements than Botania. It has a few worldgen structures, but the most notable is the Fairy Shrine: a circular pool ringed with birch and oak trees. At night, fairies spawn, although the uses for these are somewhat mysterious.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#embers" id="user-content-embers"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Embers</h3>
<p>The little bit of worldgen Embers has to offer isn't of significant note except as possible cosmetic materials to a builder, but Ancient Golems do spawn occasionally, which are a meaningful hazard to the novice adventurer. They have more health than is typical outside of mini-bosses or haunted buildings, and a reasonably hard hitting (if slow) ranged attack in the form of a fireball.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#ice-and-fire" id="user-content-ice-and-fire"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Ice and Fire</h3>
<p>While not currently in the work-in-progress pack, one of the most significant for gameflow impact is <em>Ice and Fire</em>, which adds dragons to the overworld.</p>
<p>This results in lairs, both on the surface and underground, containing both fire-breathing and ice-breathing dragons, and every last one of them is a murderous bastard with hundreds of hit points.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#tinkers-construct" id="user-content-tinkers-construct"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Tinkers' Construct</h3>
<p>While mostly a tools mod, <em>Tinkers' Construct</em> does add world generation in the form of slime islands floating in the sky. These islands are not only a good place to obtain slime blocks of the three types (green, blue, purple), they're also a place to find liquid versions of same, and Slimy Trees. When cut down, you obtain Slimy Saplings of one of the colors, which can be used as a renewable source of that type of slime. These saplings can <strong>only</strong> be placed on slimy dirt and slimy grass.</p>

<h2>

<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#the-twilight-forest" id="user-content-the-twilight-forest"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>The Twilight Forest</h3>
<p>Possibly the most expansive of our adventuring content, <em>the Twilight Forest</em> is a self-contained adventure dimension that has a fairly extensive progression system and an astonishing diversity of exploration environments and custom bosses.</p>
<p>The portal is a simple construction: a 2x2 pool of water surrounded by flowers, with a diamond tossed into the center. The far end of the portal is placed somewhere in the earliest tier biome, and you can return at any time in comparative safety, provided you haven't ventured somewhere more dangerous.</p>
<p>The Twilight Forest is teeming with many kinds of wildlife, some unique enemies, and a great deal of enticing places that can turn out to be traps. Caution is advised, although the rewards are not insubstantial.</p>

<p>While part of vanilla minecraft, the nether has some subtle alterations from the various mods present. Like the overworld, there are forestry beehives, and they hate you.</p>
<p>Blazes can spawn everywhere, not just in nether fortresses, and they hate you.</p>
<p>Tinkers' Construct has lava versions of its slime islands, which spawns magma slimes, and they hate you.</p>
<p>Quark adds Wraiths, a ghostly enemy that uses the sounds of various overworld critters, and they hate you. They also drop an item that, if used, causes untold numbers of various enemies to spawn around you, which all most especially hate you.</p>
<p>Evilcraft, among other things, adds Nether Fish to the nether, which are very similar to vanilla silverfish. The primary difference is that they reside in netherrack instead of cobblestone. They, rarely, can even turn up in the overworld wherever netherrack happens to occur. In case it still needs pointing out, they hate you.</p>

<p>Largely unmodified from vanilla.</p>
<p>There are forestry bees, and like all forestry bees, they hate you.</p>
<p>Relatively new from previous popular versions of minecraft, the End has 'cities' that are full of spite and treasure. Mostly spite. This is where you can get the 'elytra' item, something that bears a passing resemblance to a glider, but with bizarre mechanics, and taking up your chest slot for armor. (The <a href="#colytra">Colytra</a> mod alters this.)</p>

<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#akashic-tome" id="user-content-akashic-tome"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Akashic Tome</h3>
<p>This mod only adds one item, and its only purpose is to store all the books. Literally all of them. You add a book to the Tome by crafting the tome and the new book together, getting a version of the Tome with that book integrated. Right-clicking the Tome lets you pick which book you want it to become... Then punching the air with that book in hand reverts it back to its Akashic Tome state.</p>
<p>To remove a book from the Tome, select it, then hold shift while pressing the drop key (default Q)</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#backpacks" id="user-content-backpacks"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Backpacks</h3>
<p>This mod adds portable storage options. They can be stored in other mods' storage objects, but not nested within themselves (despite what the online documentation says; that functionality has been disabled).</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#chest-transporter" id="user-content-chest-transporter"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Chest Transporter</h3>
<p>Reminiscent of the 'dolly' items in mods available on previous minecraft versions, chest transporters can be used to pick up chests, storage drawers, and other storage blocks. Unlike a dolly, they have finite durability and a limited number of uses based on the materials used in their crafting. Wooden tools can only be used for a single pick up/place cycle.</p>
<p>While holding a chest transporter containing a container anywhere in your inventory, you suffer slowness and hunger debuffs. You'll want to put the containers down (or store the transporters in some other inventory) as quickly as possible.</p>

<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#colytra" id="user-content-colytra"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Colytra</h3>
<p>Vanilla minecraft added an item called the elytra that resembles a glider. Its primary issue is that it wants to take up your chest armor slot, reducing your survivability even while it increases your movement options.</p>
<p>The colytra mod allows you to 'fuse' an elytra with a chestplate, creating a wearable item with the benefits of both items.</p>
<p>Alternately, you can convert an elytra into a bauble version that can go into the body slot.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#comforts" id="user-content-comforts"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Comforts</h3>
<p>Given the hazards of night time while adventuring, it's important to have access to a sleeping bag. This mod provides such.</p>
<p>It also happens to provide a hammock for sleeping through the day, but that isn't portable.</p>
<h3>

<p>A variety of special-purpose backpacks. These have lower storage capacity than the previous mod, but are cheaper, and easier to manage in some respects. Notable is the Apiarist Backpack, which only stores Forestry bees, but has an immense capacity.</p>
<p>Forestry backpacks can be placed inside other storage objects (like Backpacks mod backpacks), but not each other.</p>

<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#morph-o-tool" id="user-content-morph-o-tool"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Morph-o-tool</h3>
<p>Much like the Akashic Tome, the Morph-o-tool is intended to hold various mod-block manipulation tools. Wrenches and the like; for the most part, these aren't terribly useful to the non-builder, but exceptions may arise.</p>
<p>Unlike the Akashic Tome, you can't manually select a tool in it: they are automatically selected while looking at a mod block -- provided you have the correct tool installed.</p>

<p>Spice of Life offers Lunch Bags and Lunch Boxes, specialized storage objects which only hold food. Notably, in addition to right-clicking to use as a container like other storage objects, you can shift-right-click these to put them in an 'open' stage, where they can be 'eaten' as if food. <p>This specialty container only holds a limited number of a limited selection of items: to start with, it has two slots, and can only hold items which cannot be stacked, such as weapons or tools. But you can configure a hotkey to open a radial menu which allows you to place your current hotbar item into an empty slot in the tool belt, pull an item from the tool belt to your current hotbar slot if it's empty, or swap the two items.</p>
<p>It can be upgraded to allow as many as 9 items in it, and the tool belt can be optionally equipped in the Belt baubles slot to free up a main inventory space.</p>
