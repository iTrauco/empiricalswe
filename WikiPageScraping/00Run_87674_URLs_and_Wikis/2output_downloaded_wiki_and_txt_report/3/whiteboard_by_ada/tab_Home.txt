          <p>#Whiteboard
Whiteboard is a course management system for schools and universities with simplicity in mind.</p>
<p>#User
Everything starts with the user. There are two types of users:</p>
<p>###Admins
powers:</p>
<ol>
<li>Create/delete/edit anything</li>
</ol>
<p>###Teacher
Powers:</p>
<ol>
<li>Create/edit/delete a course</li>
<li>Add students(aka subscribers) to his courses.</li>
<li>Add resources to his course</li>
<li>Add or remove one or more teachers to a course.</li>
</ol>
<p>Students don't need to register. Instead, their email addresses are added to the course subscribers. A student can subscribe himself to a course either by visiting the course homepage or the teacher can add him.</p>
<p>#Course
A Course has the following fields:</p>
<ol>
<li>author: the teacher who has created the course. Only the author can edit the managers list.</li>
<li>title: course title</li>
<li>description: markdown text which is shown on the course front page. Usually information like course description, course information, etc is shown here.</li>
<li>managers: a list of other teachers who manage this course</li>
<li>resources: a list of resources which belong to this course.</li>
<li>code: course code</li>
<li>period: contains information about the year and season of the course.</li>
<li>subscribers: a list of all subscribers. Subscribers(students) get notification via email whenever a resource is added, edited, or deleted. subscribers can unsubscribe anytime.</li>
<li>created: creation date</li>
</ol>
<p>#Resource
There are two types of resources and both of them can be created either by the course author or by any of the managers. A resource can have a visibility date range. For example, visible from 2014-12-12 12:12 to 2014-12-12: 23:59.</p>
<p>###Documents
Documents must have a title and markdown content. Any file or media can be uploaded, embedded and written here.</p>
<p>###Assignments
Subscribers can upload a single file. Teachers can set a due date and also report back the grades to subscribers.</p>
