<p>You must do this every time you change the database schema (that is, add or remove columns from the models).</p>
<p>Assuming the database is configured as above, open Python and run:</p>
<pre><code>from pikyak_app import *
db.drop_all()
db.create_all()
</code></pre>
<p>or execute updatedb.py</p>
<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#send-queries" id="user-content-send-queries"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Send Queries</h1>
<p>From any Bash command line, send the following:</p>
<pre><code>curl -X METHOD -d 'BODY' -H 'Content-Type: application/json' USERNAME:PASSWORD@104.131.167.43:PORT/URL
</code></pre>
<p>Replace <code>METHOD</code> with a method like PUT, POST, etc, <code>BODY</code> with valid JSON (if not required, omit <code>-d</code>); <code>USERNAME</code> and <code>PASSWORD</code> with the <em>user's</em> credentials (which may not be needed if just reading conversations or something), and <code>PORT</code> with the server's port.  <code>URL</code> is obvious I should hope.</p>
