<p>The first option of each of the tabs of the hierarchy (<a href="UserGuide.en#system">SYSTEM</a>, <a href="UserGuide.en#country">COUNTRY</a>, <a href="UserGuide.en#institution">INSTITUTION</a>, <a href="UserGuide.en#centre">CENTRE</a>, <a href="UserGuide.en#degree">DEGREE</a> and <a href="UserGuide.en#course">COURSE</a>) allows searching institutions, centres, degrees, courses, users and documents.</p>
<p>The search form contains three elements:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Scope</strong> (where to search):
<ul>
<li>System</li>
<li>Country</li>
<li>Institution</li>
<li>Centre</li>
<li>Degree</li>
<li>Course</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<strong>String to search</strong>. You can enter one or more word segments separated by spaces. The search results will contain all word segments, in any order.</li>
<li>
<strong>What to search</strong>: (the default value is recalled from one search to the next)
<ul>
<li>all (any of the following items)</li>
<li>institutions</li>
<li>centres</li>
<li>degrees</li>
<li>courses</li>
<li>users</li>
<li>teachers</li>
<li>students</li>
<li>guests (only available for system administrators)</li>
<li>open documents</li>
<li>documents in my courses (files uploaded by anyone in your courses)</li>
<li>my documents (files uploaded by you in any file area of the platform)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p>After specifying the three elements, click on the <strong>Search</strong> button and the results will be displayed.</p>
