<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#swarm-effects" id="user-content-swarm-effects"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Swarm Effects</h1>
<p>The swarm effect options work similar to abiotic factors, they modify an agent's parameter under certain conditions. Abiotic factors do this based on where the agent is in the environment, swarm does this based on how many agents are a certain distance away from this agent. Setting Radius to 0 disables the specific agent-swarm effect.</p>

<p>For swarm behaviour to occur, the AI must have a sense of how big the group of agents around it is. Using two bits allows the agent to sense the group as none/small/medium/large. Even though we will only use swarm effects on Agent 1, we must have the same number of bits in Agent 2's AI to make their behaviour comparable.</p>
<ul>
<li>Agent 1 Swarm 1 Benefit bits: 2</li>
<li>Agent 2 Swarm 2 Benefit bits: 2</li>
</ul>

<p>Swarm behaviour is very complicated compared to the agents' simple actions, so we will use learning so that the agents can learn in their lifetime instead of through generations of evolution. Again we enable it for both agents to make them comparable.</p>
<ul>
<li>Enable learning: ON for both agents</li>
</ul>

<p>Run the simulation for 10,000 ticks.
Nearly every agent dies very quickly, but Agent 2 makes a recovery around tick 5,000.
We can slow the simulation down around step 7,000 and use the Edit-&gt;Observation Mode to click on individual agents and see what kinds of paths they take.
Most of the agents do not stay in one spot, they move large distances in mostly straight lines.</p>
