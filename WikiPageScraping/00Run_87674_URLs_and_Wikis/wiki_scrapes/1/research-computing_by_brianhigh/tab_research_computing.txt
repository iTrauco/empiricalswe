<h3 id="user-content-course-goals">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#course-goals" id="user-content-course-goals"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Course Goals</h3>
<div>
<p>The main goal of this course is to help students improve their technical
readiness for engaging in the increasingly data-centric work that they face
in their degree programs, in their related research, and in their future
professional and research practice. Students will become acquainted with
key concepts in research computing and data management, including overviews
of systems analysis techniques, data security and integrity, and database
tools, among others.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-learning-objectives">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#learning-objectives" id="user-content-learning-objectives"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Learning Objectives</h3>
<div>
<p>At the end of this course students should be able to:</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Analyze requirements for management of data in different situations and projects.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Choose appropriate technical tools and techniques to support that data management.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Identify hazards and pitfalls in data-related projects.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Identify factors that affect performance in the collection and preparation of data for analysis.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Describe the core technologies most frequently employed in large-scale research data management.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="user-content-information-systems">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#information-systems" id="user-content-information-systems"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Information Systems</h2>
<div>
<div>
<p>Your research computing experience will involve using <em>and</em> developing
information systems. We will take a quick look at the various
components, types, and development models of these systems.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-information-system-components">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#information-system-components" id="user-content-information-system-components"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Information System Components</h3>
<div>
<p>The primary components of an information system are hardware, software,
data, and <em>people</em> — the most important component of all! Why? Because
systems are designed and built <em>by</em> people <em>for</em> people. If people don’t
use them, or they do not serve the people’s needs, then they are
worthless! Today we will take a closer look at how information systems
are designed to help us.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-hardware">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#hardware" id="user-content-hardware"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Hardware</h3>
<div>
<p>The physical machinery of a computer system is called its
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_hardware" rel="nofollow">hardware</a>. Of course,
this means the computer itself, its chassis and the parts inside it,
including its core
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit" rel="nofollow">integrated circuit</a>
known as the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_processing_unit" rel="nofollow">central processing
unit (CPU)</a>, as well as its memory, called "RAM" or Random Access
Memory, and any internal storage devices like hard disk drives (HDD) and
solid state devices (SSD).</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Accessories or [peripherals(<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peripheral" rel="nofollow">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peripheral</a>) are
the devices you plug into the computer, mostly for input and output.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Networking_hardware" rel="nofollow">Networking equipment</a>
includes all of the devices that allow your computer to communicate with
other systems. Examples are the network cables and the boxes they
connect to, such as routers, switches, hubs, wireless access points, and
modems.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-software">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#software" id="user-content-software"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Software</h3>
<div>
<p>Software is the name for the instructions we give to computing devices
to tell them what to do. Software is "soft" because the instructions are
not physical entities like hardware devices. The instructions may be
stored on physical media like a hard disk or USB thumbrive, just as a
cooking recipe may be written on a piece of paper or printed in a book.
However, the recipe itself is just a <em>conceptual model</em> of how to
perform a task. Likewise, a software program is essentially just a list
of instructions (or a <em>logical model</em> that issues instructions) for the
execution of a set of desired computing operations.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-application-software">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#application-software" id="user-content-application-software"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Application Software</h4>
<div>
<p>As you use a computer, the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software" rel="nofollow">software</a> instructions that are
executed on your behalf by the CPU, such as
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_program" rel="nofollow">programs</a> and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_software" rel="nofollow">apps</a>, are called
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Application_software" rel="nofollow">application software</a>.
Applications are the programs that serve a specific purpose for a
computer <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_%28computing%29" rel="nofollow">user</a> or are
to be used for completing certain tasks, such as exploring the Internet,
editing a text document, or working with data.</p>
</div>
</div>

<h4 id="user-content-system-software">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#system-software" id="user-content-system-software"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>System Software</h4>
<div>
<h5 id="user-content-the-operating-system">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#the-operating-system" id="user-content-the-operating-system"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>The Operating System</h5>
<div>
<p>Applications run within a <em>overall</em> software environment called the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system" rel="nofollow">operating system (OS)</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Notable examples are the familiar
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" rel="nofollow">Microsoft Windows</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OS_X" rel="nofollow">OS X</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IOS" rel="nofollow">iOS</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Android" rel="nofollow">Android</a> and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux" rel="nofollow">Linux</a> operating systems.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h5 id="user-content-kernel-drivers-and-firmware">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#kernel-drivers-and-firmware" id="user-content-kernel-drivers-and-firmware"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Kernel, Drivers, and Firmware</h5>
<div>
<p>An operating system also has a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kernel_(operating_system)" rel="nofollow">kernel</a>, which is
the central software program that manages the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_%28computing%29" rel="nofollow">data</a> exchange between
the CPU and the other components within a computer. The kernel
communicates with those components using
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Device_driver" rel="nofollow">device drivers</a>, which are
small programs that provide a software
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interface_(computing)" rel="nofollow">interface</a> to the
hardware. Devices that contain integrated circuits of their own may
store software in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware" rel="nofollow">firmware</a> that
allows updates through a procedure called
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Firmware#Flashing" rel="nofollow">flashing</a>. The computing
system will also contain
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utility_software" rel="nofollow">utility software</a> such as
configuration and management tools, plus shared
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_(computing)" rel="nofollow">software libraries</a>
used by both applications <em>and</em> system software.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-data">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#data" id="user-content-data"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Data</h3>
<div>
<p>Data refers to all of the information in the system. It may be stored as
raw (unprocessed) values, or may be in the form of summary tables,
plots, written documents, photographs, music, videos, or just about any
other form of information which can be digitized. Data can be <em>at rest</em>,
in which case it will probably be saved in some sort of file or may just
be occupying some bits of system memory. Data may also be <em>in motion</em>,
flowing between the <em>components</em> within a single computer system or
between <em>nodes</em> of a network. As the boundaries of an information system
will usually extend beyond computer systems, data may also reside on
scraps of paper or may only exist in a person’s mind in the form of a
thought or idea, waiting to be communicated to the rest of the
information system.</p>
</div>

<div>
<p>People are an integral part of the system. We design it, build it, use
it, maintain it, and adapt it to new uses. Our systems should serve
<em>us</em>, not the other way around. Every aspect of the system should be
designed to serve people’s needs <em>optimally</em>. But our needs vary, and so
we need various types of systems.</p>

<h3 id="user-content-information-system-types">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#information-system-types" id="user-content-information-system-types"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Information System Types</h3>
<div>
<p>Most of us are very familiar <em>search information systems</em> like web
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_search_engine" rel="nofollow">search</a>
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_engine_%28computing%29" rel="nofollow">engines</a>,
such as <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Search" rel="nofollow">Google Search</a>, but
many sites use domain-specific search engines like
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PubMed" rel="nofollow">PubMed</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographic_information_system" rel="nofollow">Spatial</a>
information systems</em> in the form of
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographic_information_system" rel="nofollow">Geographic
information system (GIS)</a> have become increasingly important in recent
years. <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ArcGIS" rel="nofollow">ArcGIS</a> has dominated this
field, with the free and open <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QGIS" rel="nofollow">QGIS</a>
gaining in popularity.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_information_system" rel="nofollow">Global</a>
information systems</em> (GLIS) are those either developed or used in a
global context. Public health examples include global health databases
such as the <a href="http://www.unhcr.org/pages/49c3646c4d6.html" rel="nofollow">UNHCR</a>
Statistics &amp; Operational Data Portals and the WHO’s
<a href="http://www.who.int/gho/en/" rel="nofollow">Global Health Observatory (GHO)</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enterprise_systems" rel="nofollow">Enterprise</a> systems</em> are
comprehensive organization-wide applications used for
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enterprise_resource_planning" rel="nofollow">Enterprise
Resource Planning (ERP)</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expert_systems" rel="nofollow">Expert</a> systems</em> support such
specialty domains as diagnosis, forecasting, and delivery scheduling.
They use artificial intelligence to apply knowledge and reasoning in
order to solve complex problems.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_automation" rel="nofollow">Office</a> automation
systems</em> refer to systems which support the everyday business operations
of an organization.
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_process_automation" rel="nofollow">Business
Process Automation (BPA)</a> uses these systems to improve efficiency by
streamlining routine activities.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_information_manager" rel="nofollow">Personal</a>
information systems</em> help people manage their individual communications,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calendaring_software" rel="nofollow">calendaring</a>,
note-taking, diet, and fitness.</p>
</div>

<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>

<h3 id="user-content-software-development-process">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#software-development-process" id="user-content-software-development-process"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Software Development Process</h3>
<div>
<p>Developers undertake the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_development_process" rel="nofollow">software
development process</a> using several different approaches. Let’s take a
look at a few of the most popular models.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="Software Development Types" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/Three_software_development_patterns_mashed_together.svg/507px-Three_software_development_patterns_mashed_together.svg.png" height="320" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/8f742f568b85559edfe178b184c977cac9955b9e/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f7468756d622f352f35662f54687265655f736f6674776172655f646576656c6f706d656e745f7061747465726e735f6d61736865645f746f6765746865722e7376672f35303770782d54687265655f736f6674776172655f646576656c6f706d656e745f7061747465726e735f6d61736865645f746f6765746865722e7376672e706e67"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Three_software_development_patterns_mashed_together.svg" rel="nofollow">Beao
Old waterfall: Paul Smith, Public Domain, (Wikimedia)</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p>Here is a short list of common development models. We have provided
links from each of these to relevant Wikipedia pages. You are encouraged
to read more about them. We’ll just go through the list quickly to give
you a rough idea of the differences between them.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle" rel="nofollow">Systems
development life cycle (SDLC)</a> is the classic model. It involves lots of
up-front planning and is risk averse.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterfall_model" rel="nofollow">Waterfall development</a> is
another and "old school" favorite, It’s like the SDLC but does not offer
any sort of feedback loop.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_prototyping" rel="nofollow">Prototyping</a> is a
useful technique for many models. Good when a small-scale experiment can
prove an idea without risking heavy investment.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iterative_and_incremental_development" rel="nofollow">Iterative
and incremental development</a> might evoke the image of "baby steps" or
the notion of "try, try, again". There is a central loop, between
initial planning and final deployment, which repeats as needed. Like
prototyping, it is a technique which can be used in other models.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Likewise, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spiral_model" rel="nofollow">Spiral development</a>
is meant to address evolving requirements through cycles of repeated
analysis and design, getting closer and closer to the desired product.
The idea is that the <em>entire process</em> is repeated over and over until
you are finally satisfied.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapid_application_development" rel="nofollow">Rapid
application development (RAD)</a> focuses on development more than
up-front planning.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agile_software_development" rel="nofollow">Agile
development</a> is a more evolved form of RAD, with more of a focus on user
engagement, and gaining wide popularity.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cowboy_coding" rel="nofollow">Code and fix</a> sounds like
what it is — <em>cowboy coding</em> — what most lone programmers do, and what
might seem most familiar to you as a scientific researcher. This can be
quick for easy projects, but can be very inefficient and expensive for
larger projects, due to insufficient planning.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>They are all useful methods, though, some more generally than others.
The approach you take should depend upon your situation.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>We’ll look more closely at three of these right now.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_development_process" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-development-life-cycle" id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Development Life Cycle</h4>
<div>
<p>Since information systems are so complex, it is very helpful to follow a
standard development model to make sure you take care of all of the
little details without missing any.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="SDLC cycle" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/SDLC-Maintenance-Highlighted.png/360px-SDLC-Maintenance-Highlighted.png" height="280" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/543cb1d3d6644ae1129d5004bb2de4214452890d/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f7468756d622f372f37652f53444c432d4d61696e74656e616e63652d486967686c6967687465642e706e672f33363070782d53444c432d4d61696e74656e616e63652d486967686c6967687465642e706e67"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:SDLC-Maintenance-Highlighted.png" rel="nofollow">Dzonatas,
CC BY-SA 3.0, (Wikimedia)</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>For years, the standard development model was known as the SDLC, or
Systems Development Life Cycle. It works well for large, complex,
expensive projects, but can be scaled down as needed. Many of its phases
are used in the other models as well. Let’s take a quick look at them.</p>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle" rel="nofollow">Systems
development life cycle</a> (SDLC)
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#Phases" rel="nofollow">phases</a>:</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_investigation" rel="nofollow">Planning</a>
(<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feasibility_study" rel="nofollow">feasibility study</a>)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>There is a focus on careful <em>planning</em> before any design or coding
takes place. The feasibility study explores your options and gaining
approval from stakeholders.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_analysis" rel="nofollow">Analysis</a></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Analysis</em> includes a detailed study of the current system and
clearly identifying requirements before designing a new system.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#Design" rel="nofollow">Design</a></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Once you have thoroughly defined the requirements, you can begin to
model the new system.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implementation#Information_Technology_.28IT.29" rel="nofollow">Implementation</a></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Implementation</em> is where the hardware assembly, software coding,
testing, and deployment takes place.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_maintenance" rel="nofollow">Maintenance</a></p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Maintenance</em> may sound boring, but it is essential to ensure that
the project is an overall success.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>The main idea is that systems development is a cycle — a continual
process. You need to allow for maintenance, updates, and new features.
The use and upkeep of the system provides feedback which goes into
planning the next version.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>We will spend more time on the SDLC and its early phases in a separate
module.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
</div>

<h4 id="user-content-waterfall-model">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#waterfall-model" id="user-content-waterfall-model"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Waterfall Model</h4>
<div>
<p>A related model is the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterfall_model" rel="nofollow">Waterfall model</a>. It has
basically same same steps as the SDLC, but visualizes them as cascading
stair-steps instead of a circle.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="Waterfall model" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Waterfall_model.svg/640px-Waterfall_model.svg.png" height="280" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/aa0a9c64afe0514a684a6786bd85fcfcf8d103b3/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f7468756d622f652f65322f576174657266616c6c5f6d6f64656c2e7376672f36343070782d576174657266616c6c5f6d6f64656c2e7376672e706e67"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Waterfall_model.svg" rel="nofollow">Peter Kemp /
Paul Smith, CC BY 3.0, (Wikimedia)</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>It’s basically similar to the SDLC, but without the feedback loop. There
are cascading stair-steps, where one phase leads to another and the
output of one phase becomes sthe input of another. Its came from
manufactoring where after-the-fact changes are expensive or impossible.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waterfall_model" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-agile-model">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#agile-model" id="user-content-agile-model"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Agile Model</h4>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agile_software_development" rel="nofollow">The Agile model</a>
is a newer, but very popular, especially among smaller teams within
budding organizations. Hallmarks of this model include methods such as
pair programming, test-driven development, and frequent product
releases.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="Agile Software Development methodology" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Agile_Software_Development_methodology.jpg/387px-Agile_Software_Development_methodology.jpg" height="320" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/159d9d768b800536d2b68af3415206660ee148e5/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f7468756d622f362f36632f4167696c655f536f6674776172655f446576656c6f706d656e745f6d6574686f646f6c6f67792e6a70672f33383770782d4167696c655f536f6674776172655f446576656c6f706d656e745f6d6574686f646f6c6f67792e6a7067"/></span></p>

<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Agile_Software_Development_methodology.jpg" rel="nofollow">VersionOne,
Inc., CC BY-SA 3.0, (Wikimedia)</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Smaller teams that can meet regularly, ideally face-to-face. Working in
pairs, with one person coding and other helping "over the shoulder".
After you identify use cases, then you write tests and then build the
system to pass the tests. By developing an automated test and build
system, releases can be pushed out quickly and more often.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agile_software_development" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,

<h4 id="user-content-transparency">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#transparency" id="user-content-transparency"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Transparency</h4>
<div>
<p>Information systems vary in the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_system_%28computing%29" rel="nofollow">openness</a> of
the their implementations, in terms of both
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interoperability" rel="nofollow">interoperability</a>
standards and specific design details.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>You can have <em>open</em> systems and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_standard" rel="nofollow">standards</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source" rel="nofollow">source</a>), where the technical
specifications are publicly available. Different organizations may
implement them in their own way, yet still maintain
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interoperability" rel="nofollow">interoperability</a> with
other implementations.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Or systems be <em>closed</em>, or
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proprietary_software" rel="nofollow">proprietary</a>, where an
organization keeps the details to itself, making it more difficult for
competitors to inter-operate. While this may provide a competitive
advantage for the producer it contributes to what is called
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vendor_lock-in" rel="nofollow">vendor lock-in</a>, where a
consumer becomes dependent on the vendor, unable to switch to another
due to the high costs and disruption.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>These interoperability aspects will include
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File_format" rel="nofollow">file formats</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communications_protocol" rel="nofollow">communications
protocols</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_security" rel="nofollow">security</a>
and <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encryption" rel="nofollow">encryption</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_standard#Open_v._closed_standards" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>All of those are important when you are collaborating, sharing data and
files with others, who might be using different platforms.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>By using transparent systems, you not only increase your ease of
communication and collaboration, you also contribute to openness in a
broader, social context.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Information
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transparency_%28behavior%29" rel="nofollow">transparency</a>
supports <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Openness" rel="nofollow">openness</a> in:</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_government" rel="nofollow">Government</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_research" rel="nofollow">Research</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_education" rel="nofollow">Education</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenCourseWare" rel="nofollow">Courseware</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_content" rel="nofollow">Content</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_culture_movement" rel="nofollow">Culture</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>We have provided links to several popular movements which are working to
increase openness and transparency in various aspects of society. You
are encouraged to spend some time learning about these trends.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>So, if you want the benefits of openness in your work and more freedom
to make changes, consider building your information infrastructure with
open technologies.</p>
</div>

<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>

<h2 id="user-content-systems-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-analysis" id="user-content-systems-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Analysis</h2>
<div>
<div>
<p>Investing time and money into a computer or information system without a
clear course of action can be expensive and wasteful. By taking some
time to fully consider the issue at hand and pursue a disciplined
approach to finding a practical solution, you greatly increase your odds
of success and decrease costs. We call this process <em>Systems Analysis</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-development-life-cycle-1" id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle-1"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Development Life Cycle</h3>
<div>
<p>Systems analysis is an important part of an overall approach to <em>systems
development</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The life of an information system follows a cycle. The classic
development model is called the Systems Development Life Cycle, or SDLC.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-planning-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#planning-phase" id="user-content-planning-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Planning Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_investigation" rel="nofollow">Planning</a>
phase defines the primary issue (<em>problem</em> or <em>goal</em>) and performs a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feasibility_study" rel="nofollow">feasibility study</a>. Here,
you clarify the project scope, compare your best options, and come up
with a plan.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-analysis-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#analysis-phase" id="user-content-analysis-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Analysis Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_analysis" rel="nofollow">Analysis</a>
phase focusses on the issue, defined previously, and studies its role in
the current (or proposed) system. The system is explored, piece by
piece, in light of the project goals, to determine system requirements.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-design-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#design-phase" id="user-content-design-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Design Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>In the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#Design" rel="nofollow">Design</a>
phase, a detailed model of the proposed system is created. Various
components or modules address each of the requirements identified
earlier.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-implementation-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#implementation-phase" id="user-content-implementation-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Implementation Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>During the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implementation#Information_Technology_.28IT.29" rel="nofollow">Implementation</a>
phase, a working system is built from the design and put into use.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-maintenance-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#maintenance-phase" id="user-content-maintenance-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Maintenance Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_maintenance" rel="nofollow">Maintenance</a> phase
includes ongoing updates and evaluation. As changes are needed, the
cycle repeats with more planning, analysis, and so on.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-what-will-you-need">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#what-will-you-need" id="user-content-what-will-you-need"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>What will you need?</h3>
<div>
<p>Essentially, in <em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems
analysis</a></em> we answer the question, "<em>What will you need</em> to reach your
goal?" In other words, <strong>"What are your requirements?"</strong></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A <strong>primary goal</strong> of this course is to help you develop your skills in
<em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">requirements
analysis</a></em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> helps
you <strong>clarify your project needs</strong> and <strong>plan ahead</strong> in order to <em>obtain
and allocate</em> <strong>critical resources</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The main idea here is …​</p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p>If you don’t <em>know</em> what you <em>need</em>, how can you <em>ask</em> for it?</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
<div>

<div>
<p>After completing an initial
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feasibility_study" rel="nofollow">feasibility study</a> to
"determine if creating a new or improved system is a viable solution",
proposing the project, and gaining approval from
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_stakeholder" rel="nofollow">stakeholders</a>, you may
then conduct a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">systems
analysis</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> will
involve "http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Work_breakdown_structure[breaking
down] the system in different pieces to analyze the situation,
<strong>analyzing project goals</strong>, breaking down what needs to be created and
attempting to <strong>engage users</strong> so that
<strong><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">definite
requirements</a></strong> can be defined."</p>
</div>
<div>

<div>
<p>So, what, exactly, <em>is</em> "Systems Analysis"?</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>"A <em>system</em> is a set of interacting or interdependent components"<sup>1</sup></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Analysis</em> means "to take apart"<sup>2</sup></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>Lonnie D. Bentley, author of <em>Systems Analysis and Design for the Global
Enterprise</em>, writes:</p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> is a
problem solving technique that decomposes a system into its component
pieces for the purpose of the studying how well those component parts
work and interact to accomplish their purpose.<sup>3</sup></p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
<div>
<p>We will accomplish this analysis by working through a series of
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis#Information_technology" rel="nofollow">five
phases</a>.</p>
</div>

<div>
<p>Systems Analysis has its own series of phases. We will look at each of
these one by one.</p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<img alt="systems analysis phases" height="563" src="images/systems_analysis_01.png" width="352"/>
</div>
<div></div>
</div>
<div>
<p>During <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scope_%28project_management%29" rel="nofollow">Scope
definition</a>, we establish our system boundaries. In our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_statement" rel="nofollow">Problem analysis</a> phase,
we identify the symptoms and causes. Our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">Requirements
analysis</a> determines our system goals. The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_design#Logical_design" rel="nofollow">Logical
design</a> phase models relationships within the system. And our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_analysis" rel="nofollow">Decision analysis</a>
evaluates the various alternatives.</p>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-scope-definition">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#scope-definition" id="user-content-scope-definition"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Scope Definition</h3>
<div>
<p>So, do we mean by <em>scope</em>?<sup>1</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><strong>Scope</strong> involves <em>getting information</em> required to start a project, and
the <em>features</em> the product would need to have in order to meet its
<em>stakeholders requirements</em>.</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
<div>
<p>Put another way, <em>project</em> scope defines the <em>work to be done</em> whereas
<em>product</em> scope is concerned with the <em>desired features and functions</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>By being careful to define scope <em>early on</em>, we can be more watchful for
<em>scope creep</em>.<sup>2</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><strong>Scope creep</strong> is […​] the <em>incremental expansion</em> of the scope of a
project […​], while nevertheless <em>failing to adjust</em> the schedule and
budget.</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-problem-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#problem-analysis" id="user-content-problem-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Problem Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>Problem analysis is critical. When we say, <em>problem</em>, we can also think
<em>goal</em>, <em>research question</em>, or <em>issue</em>. If you don’t get this right,
you can waste a lot of time and money solving the <em>wrong problem</em> or
address a <em>non-issue</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>We can summarize the key points of problem analysis (with some help from
Jenette Nagy of Kansas University) as:</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Define and clarify the problem (or issue)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What exactly are we trying to solve?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Determine the problem’s importance</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>How much does it matter?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Assess the feasibility of solving the problem</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Do we have the resources we need?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Consider any negative impacts (unintended consequences)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What could go wrong?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Prioritize problems to solve (bottlenecks? low-hanging fruit?)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What are the most critical issues?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Answer: <em>what</em>, <em>why</em>, <em>who</em>, <em>when</em>, <em>where</em>, and <em>how much</em>?</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Drill down into the problem with all kinds of questions to expose
dependencies.</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Find <strong>causes</strong> (especially
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_cause_analysis" rel="nofollow">root cause</a>) and
<strong>symptoms</strong> (effects)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What is really going on here?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source:
<a href="http://ctb.ku.edu/en/table-of-contents/analyze/analyze-community-problems-and-solutions/define-analyze-problem/main" rel="nofollow">Jenette
Nagy, Kansas University, CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 US</a></span></em></p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-root-cause-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#root-cause-analysis" id="user-content-root-cause-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Root Cause Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>There are several methods of root cause analysis, but one simple one is
…​</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em>"Ask Why Five Times"</em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="Ask Why Five Times" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/20/Root_Cause_Analysis_Tree_Diagram.jpg" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/319f6c4fd47f0959786de2b490419de836ad249e/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f322f32302f526f6f745f43617573655f416e616c797369735f547265655f4469616772616d2e6a7067"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>… where you keep asking "Why? But, why? But, Why?" over and over again
until there is a clear root cause that you can actually do something
about.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A real example is mentioned in
<a href="http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Business_Analysis_Guidebook/Root_Cause_Analysis#Ask_Why_5_Times" rel="nofollow">Ask
Why 5 Times, Business Analysis Guidebook/Root Cause Analysis
(wikibooks.org)</a>, where the US National Park Service was able to slow
the rate of deterioration of the Jefferson memorial simply by changing
the lighting schedule.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>This method may also be used in Requirements Analysis.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source:
<a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Root_Cause_Analysis_Tree_Diagram.jpg" rel="nofollow">KellyLawless,
CC BY-SA 3.0 Unported (Wikimedia)</a> See also:
<a href="http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Business_Analysis_Guidebook/Root_Cause_Analysis#Ask_Why_5_Times" rel="nofollow">Ask
Why 5 Times, Business Analysis Guidebook/Root Cause Analysis
(Wikibooks)</a></span></em></p>
</div>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-requirements-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-analysis" id="user-content-requirements-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Analysis</h3>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Overview" rel="nofollow">Elicit,
Analyze, and Record (EAR)</a>:</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>System and project
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">requirements</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>EAR Analysis is all about <em>listening</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Gather requirements by interviewing stakeholders. Observe how people
currently do their work or use the system. Make sure the requirements
are detailed, clear, unambigious, and comprehensive. Document the
requirements as a list, in diagrams, or narratives.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>As an example, in Spring 2014, we held a meeting with our graduate
students and asked them about what sort of computing needs they had. We
had a discussion, <em>listened</em> to what they had to say, summarized the
main concerns, and documented them.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Further elucidate
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Measurable_goals" rel="nofollow">Measurable
goals</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>By continuing to ask questions like "Why? …​ Why? …​ Why? …​", get
more specific until the goals become quantifiable. Measured goals are
easier to meet since you can measure your progress in achieving them.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Mission objectives determine the goals. Compare the stated goals against
mission objectives to produce a small set of critical, measured goals.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Output:
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_specification" rel="nofollow">Requirements
specification</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>This document will be used in the <em>logical design</em> phase to model the
relationships within the system. So, it should be clear and thorough.
The more specific and unambiguous this specification is, the easier it
will be to design the system to meet the requirements.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>One way to be more clear is to organize the requirements by type.</p>
</div>
<div>

<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>

<h4 id="user-content-requirements-categories">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-categories" id="user-content-requirements-categories"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Categories</h4>
<div>
<p>You may categorize requirements according to several important types:</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Types_of_Requirements" rel="nofollow">Types
of requirements</a>:</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Operational requirements are the <strong>utility, effectiveness, and
deployment</strong> needs, with repect to practical use of the system within the
overall operation. These are the <strong>customer Requirements</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_requirement" rel="nofollow">Functional</a>
requirements are specific things the system must <strong>do</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-functional_requirement" rel="nofollow">Non-functional</a>
requirements are specific things the system must <strong>provide</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_architecture" rel="nofollow">Architectural</a>
requirements define how the system must be <strong>structured</strong>, in other words,
how the components must <strong>interrelate</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Behavioral requirements describe how <strong>users</strong> and other systems will
<strong>interact</strong> with the system and how the <strong>system</strong> will respond.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Performance requirements define <strong>how well</strong> the systems needs to do
things, <strong>measured</strong> in terms of quantity, quality, coverage, timeliness
or readiness.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>While there are other ways to group requirements, these are the most
significant categories.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-requirements-modeling-example">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-modeling-example" id="user-content-requirements-modeling-example"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Modeling: Example</h3>
<div>
<p>Another way to make requirements clear is through <em>requirements
modeling</em>. Here is an example of modeling behavioral requirements with a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Use_case" rel="nofollow">Use Case Diagram</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><strong>Survey Data System</strong></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The behavioral requirements are stated in <strong>role</strong> <em>goal</em> format.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Researcher</strong> <em>uploads survey</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Subject</strong> <em>takes survey</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Subject</strong> <em>uploads results</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Researcher</strong> <em>downloads results</em>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>Gramatically speaking, the role is the <em>subject</em> and the goal is stated
as a <em>verb</em> and its <em>object</em>. Each of these requirements forms the basis
of a <em>use case</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-use-case-diagram">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#use-case-diagram" id="user-content-use-case-diagram"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Use Case Diagram</h4>
<div>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Use_case" rel="nofollow">Use Case Diagram</a> is a
standard means of showing these requirements pictorially.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="research survey data system" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/brianhigh/data-workshop/master/images/research_survey_data_system.png"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source:
<a href="https://github.com/brianhigh/data-workshop/blob/master/images/research_survey_data_system.png">Brian
High, Github</a>, <a href="http://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/" rel="nofollow">Public
Domain, CC0 1.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Here, human <em>actors</em> are depicted as stick figures. These are the
<strong>roles</strong>. The <em>goal</em> is the line connecting the role to a system activity
or function, shown as an oval. In this example, the <em>system</em> is drawn as
a box with the actors outside the box. The reason is that the system’s
<em>product scope</em> was defined to be the electronic data system. The human
actors interact with that system. The information flowing to and from an
actor, as well as data flows within a system, will be modeled in the
<em>logical design</em>.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-logical-design">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#logical-design" id="user-content-logical-design"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Logical Design</h3>
<div>
<p>Once system behaviours have been modeled from the perspective of user
interaction, we can begin to model the internal workings of the system
with the <em>logical design</em>. The <em>logical</em> part means that this is an
<em>abstract representation</em> of the system. Therefore, the system is
modeled in terms of abstractions such as <em>data flows, entities, and
relationships</em>. We model how the system will satisfy function
requirements such as <em>inputs and outputs</em>. To make the abstract more
concrete, we make use of <em>Graphical modeling</em> techniques to produce
graphics such as the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_flow_diagram" rel="nofollow">Data
Flow Diagram (DFD)</a> and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity%E2%80%93relationship_model" rel="nofollow">Entity
Relationship Diagram (ERD)</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source:
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_design#Logical_design" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd" id="user-content-example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Example Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)</h4>
<div>
<p>For example, let’s consider a systems which records the playlists of
musical performances at a local pub. You will want to store which artist
performs which song. The relationship between an artist and a song can
be shown in a simple
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity%E2%80%93relationship_model" rel="nofollow">Entity
Relationship Diagram</a>. We start with the behavioral <em>use case</em> written
as <strong>Artist</strong> <em>performs Song</em>. Then, using a standard set of symbols, we
can produce this diagram.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><span><img alt="artist performs song" data-canonical-src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/ERD-artist-performs-song.svg/640px-ERD-artist-performs-song.svg.png" src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/186a0d754d0b293c0101198d1b58ac8fb267244b/687474703a2f2f75706c6f61642e77696b696d656469612e6f72672f77696b6970656469612f636f6d6d6f6e732f7468756d622f392f39312f4552442d6172746973742d706572666f726d732d736f6e672e7376672f36343070782d4552442d6172746973742d706572666f726d732d736f6e672e7376672e706e67"/></span></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The boxes represent <em>entities</em> and the line connecting them is the
relationship. Here, the symbols indicate that there is a one-to-many
relationship. This diagram is saying that exactly one artist performs
one or more songs, (or doesn’t perform any).</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Since this "system" describes only solo performers, you would want to
model it differently to include groups of artists that perform more than
one song. You would want a many-to-many relationship as well as other
entities to represent the musical groups. Since there will by many
performances, you will also want an entity to represent the
performances.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The value of this type of diagram is that complex relationships can be
mapped to a great level of detail. In fact, they can be used to
automatically generate database schemas.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source:
<a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ERD-artist-performs-song.svg" rel="nofollow">Bignose,
Wikimedia</a>, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_domain" rel="nofollow">Public Domain</a></span></em></p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-decision-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#decision-analysis" id="user-content-decision-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Decision Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>These models and diagrams will help you make a very important decision.
Before you go further in the system design process, you need to decide
whether to buy a system or build one. Or you may consider building a
system of components, some of which you might buy and others might be
custom built. You will want to come up with a few of the best
alternatives and present them to your stakeholders. In your case that
might be your lab manager, principal investigator, or funding agency.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Your presentation will also include a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_analysis" rel="nofollow">Decision analysis</a> to
weigh<br/>
the pros and cons of the various options against the requirements in
order to help the stakeholders with their decision. You should conclude
your analysis with a recommendation of your top choice and explain why
this choice is the most compelling. Then you will want to get a
decision, and the approval to continue, before you invest any more time
on further analysis.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The system development life cycle (SDLC) continues on to other phases,
which we do not have time to cover here. However, we hope that this
glimpse at the systems analysis phase has demonstrated the value of this
approach in gathering and clarifying requirements that can be used to
design and build an information system.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><em><span>Source: <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_analysis" rel="nofollow">Wikipedia</a>,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" rel="nofollow">CC
BY-SA 3.0</a></span></em></p>
</div>

<h2 id="user-content-buzzwords">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#buzzwords" id="user-content-buzzwords"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Buzzwords</h2>
<div>
<div>
<p>The technology world suffers from excessive use of buzzwords (words
associated with hype, or "buzz"). Someone coins a term to describe a
looming problem or seemingly magical solution. The term or phrase gains
momentum with media exposure, and soon you see it everywhere.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Yet, often, people are unsure about what the term actually means or if it
really matters. Relentless exposure to the ill-defined hype results in
the modern malady known as <em>buzzword fatigue</em>. We will help clarify a
few of them by peering through the clouds.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-cloud-computing">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#cloud-computing" id="user-content-cloud-computing"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Cloud Computing</h3>
<div>
<p>The notion of "the cloud" in computing comes from the use of a cloud
symbol in network diagrams. The cloud symbol represents the "rest of the
network" or the part of the network lying outside of your organization or
your network diagram. Usually, this "outside" means the Internet. So,
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloud_computing" rel="nofollow">cloud computing</a> is using
servers outside of your organization, often over the Internet. As you
may realize, this is not really that new, but more of a increasing
trend.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>You now have several competing services to choose from that present vast
computing resources as a "commodity", like electricity. Computing
resources may be rented on an as-needed basis to scale to meet varying
demand. You only pay for the resources you use. You can even run your
own <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_machine" rel="nofollow">virtual machine</a> in the
cloud, through the wonders of
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtualization" rel="nofollow">virtualization</a>, a buzzword
with its own <a href="http://virtualowl.kennesaw.edu/buzzwords.html" rel="nofollow">family of
buzzwords</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>So, the attraction is not having to buy, house, and maintain hardware,
networking, and in some cases, software. The potential downsides all
relate to "lack of control".</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong><em>Security</em></strong>: You have to trust the cloud service provider with
your data.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong><em>Support</em></strong>: You have to go through the cloud service provider
if something isn’t working the way you expect it to work.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong><em>Availability</em></strong>: You have to depend on your network connection
to the cloud service, in terms of both reliability and performance.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>Some people consider "the cloud" as just another name for "the
network" or "not my computer". In any case, the notion of <em>cloud
computing</em> involves some degree of "outsourcing" of computing resources
and accessing those resources through the network.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-big-data">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#big-data" id="user-content-big-data"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Big Data</h3>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_data" rel="nofollow">Big Data</a> is a term applied to
very large amounts of data. Search online and you will find many
different ways to define this. For our purposes, we will use the broad
definition of: a collection of data so large that its largeness creates
significant processing problems, but can yield value not found in
smaller data sets.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Very large data sets are enabled by very low cost storage. For example,
instead of sampling every hour or even every minute, you can sample
every second because you have the space to store all of the resultant
data.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-data-mining">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#data-mining" id="user-content-data-mining"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Data Mining</h3>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_mining" rel="nofollow">Data mining</a> is looking for
patterns in a very large data set. Imagine trying to find the "needles
in a haystack" of huge volumes of data. Much of this is enabled by very
low cost, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parallel_computing" rel="nofollow">parallel
computing</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The limiting factor here becomes the network because you can only move
so much data in a given time frame. Here you may turn to <em>cloud
computing</em> for
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massively_parallel_%28computing%29" rel="nofollow">
massively parallel</a> processing power.</p>
</div>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-data-science">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#data-science" id="user-content-data-science"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Data Science</h3>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_science" rel="nofollow">Data science</a> is a term
generally used to refer to statistical data analysis and the
presentation of results, often visually. In particular, some people use
"data science" to mean the analysis of <em>big data</em> using techniques such
as <em>data mining</em> to produce stunning
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_visualization" rel="nofollow">visualizations</a>. The
publishing of such <em>data products</em> is termed
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_journalism" rel="nofollow">data journalism</a>. Of
course, the analysis may make use of <em>cloud computing</em> in terms of cloud
storage and processing.</p>
</div>
