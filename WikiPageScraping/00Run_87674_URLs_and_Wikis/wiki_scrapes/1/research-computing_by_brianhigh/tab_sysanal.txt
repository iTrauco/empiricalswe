<h2 id="user-content-systems-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-analysis" id="user-content-systems-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Analysis</h2>
<div>
<div>
<p>Investing time and money into a computer or information system without a
clear course of action can be expensive and wasteful. By taking some
time to fully consider the issue at hand and pursue a disciplined
approach to finding a practical solution, you greatly increase your odds
of success and decrease costs. We call this process <em>Systems Analysis</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle-sysanal">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-development-life-cycle" id="user-content-systems-development-life-cycle"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Development Life Cycle</h3>
<div>
<p>Systems analysis is an important part of an overall approach to <em>systems
development</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The life of an information system follows a cycle. The classic
development model is called the Systems Development Life Cycle, or
SDLC.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_1" id="user-content-_footnoteref_1" title="View footnote.">1</a>]</sup></p>
</div>

</div>

<h4 id="user-content-planning-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#planning-phase" id="user-content-planning-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Planning Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_investigation" rel="nofollow">Planning</a>
phase defines the primary issue (<em>problem</em> or <em>goal</em>) and performs a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feasibility_study" rel="nofollow">feasibility study</a>. Here,
you clarify the project scope, compare your best options, and come up
with a plan.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-analysis-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#analysis-phase" id="user-content-analysis-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Analysis Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#System_analysis" rel="nofollow">Analysis</a>
phase focusses on the issue, defined previously, and studies its role in
the current (or proposed) system. The system is explored, piece by
piece, in light of the project goals, to determine system requirements.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-design-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#design-phase" id="user-content-design-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Design Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>In the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_development_life_cycle#Design" rel="nofollow">Design</a>
phase, a detailed model of the proposed system is created. Various
components or modules address each of the requirements identified
earlier.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-implementation-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#implementation-phase" id="user-content-implementation-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Implementation Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>During the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implementation#Information_Technology_.28IT.29" rel="nofollow">Implementation</a>
phase, a working system is built from the design and put into use.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-maintenance-phase">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#maintenance-phase" id="user-content-maintenance-phase"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Maintenance Phase</h4>
<div>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_maintenance" rel="nofollow">Maintenance</a> phase
includes ongoing updates and evaluation. As changes are needed, the
cycle repeats with more planning, analysis, and so on. We will take a closer look at the
<strong><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">systems analysis</a></strong> phase
next.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-what-will-you-need">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#what-will-you-need" id="user-content-what-will-you-need"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>What will you need?</h3>
<div>
<p>Essentially, in <em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems
analysis</a></em> we answer the question, "<em>What will you need</em> to reach your
goal?" In other words, <strong>"What are your requirements?"</strong></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A <strong>primary goal</strong> of this course is to help you develop your skills in
<em><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">requirements
analysis</a></em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> helps
you <strong>clarify your project needs</strong> and <strong>plan ahead</strong> in order to <em>obtain
and allocate</em> <strong>critical resources</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The main idea here is …​</p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p>If you don’t <em>know</em> what you <em>need</em>, how can you <em>ask</em> for it?</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-systems-analysis-1">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-analysis-1" id="user-content-systems-analysis-1"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>After completing an initial
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feasibility_study" rel="nofollow">feasibility study</a> to
"determine if creating a new or improved system is a viable solution",
proposing the project, and gaining approval from
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_stakeholder" rel="nofollow">stakeholders</a>, you may
then conduct a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">systems
analysis</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> will
involve …​</p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Work_breakdown_structure" rel="nofollow">breaking
down</a> the system in different pieces to analyze the situation,
<strong>analyzing project goals</strong>, breaking down what needs to be created and
attempting to <strong>engage users</strong> so that
<strong><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">definite
requirements</a></strong> can be defined.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_2" id="user-content-_footnoteref_2" title="View footnote.">2</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
</blockquote>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-systems-analysis-definition">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-analysis-definition" id="user-content-systems-analysis-definition"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Analysis Definition</h3>
<div>
<p>So, what, exactly, <em>is</em> "Systems Analysis"?</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>"A <em>system</em> is a set of interacting or interdependent components"<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_3" id="user-content-_footnoteref_3" title="View footnote.">3</a>]</sup></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><em>Analysis</em> means "to take apart"<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_4" id="user-content-_footnoteref_4" title="View footnote.">4</a>]</sup></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>Putting these two ideas together, we have:</p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis" rel="nofollow">Systems analysis</a> is a
problem solving technique that decomposes a system into its component
pieces for the purpose of the studying how well those component parts
work and interact to accomplish their purpose.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_5" id="user-content-_footnoteref_5" title="View footnote.">5</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
</blockquote>
<div>
— Lonnie D. Bentley<br/>
<cite>Systems Analysis and Design for the Global Enterprise</cite>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<p>We perform this analysis by working through a series of
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_analysis#Information_technology" rel="nofollow">five
phases</a>.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-systems-analysis-phases">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#systems-analysis-phases" id="user-content-systems-analysis-phases"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Systems Analysis Phases</h3>
<div>
<p>Systems Analysis<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_6" id="user-content-_footnoteref_6" title="View footnote.">6</a>]</sup> has its own series of phases. We will look at each of these
one by one.</p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<a href="https://github.com/brianhigh/research-computing/"><img alt="Systems Analysis Phases" height="394" src="images/systems_analysis_01.png" width="246"/></a>
</div>
<div>Systems Analysis Phases</div>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p>During <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scope_%28project_management%29" rel="nofollow">Scope
definition</a>, we establish our system boundaries. In our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Problem_statement" rel="nofollow">Problem analysis</a> phase,
we identify the symptoms and causes. Our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">Requirements
analysis</a> determines our system goals. The
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_design#Logical_design" rel="nofollow">Logical
design</a> phase models relationships within the system. And our
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_analysis" rel="nofollow">Decision analysis</a>
evaluates the various alternatives.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-scope-definition">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#scope-definition" id="user-content-scope-definition"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Scope Definition</h3>
<div>
<p>So, do we mean by <em>scope</em>?<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_7" id="user-content-_footnoteref_7" title="View footnote.">7</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><strong>Scope</strong> involves <em>getting information</em> required to start a project, and
the <em>features</em> the product would need to have in order to meet its
<em>stakeholders requirements</em>.</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
<div>
— Wikipedia<br/>
<cite>Scope (project_management)</cite>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<p>Put another way, <em>project</em> scope defines the <em>work to be done</em> whereas
<em>product</em> scope is concerned with the <em>desired features and functions</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>By being careful to define scope <em>early on</em>, we can be more watchful for
<em>scope creep</em>.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_8" id="user-content-_footnoteref_8" title="View footnote.">8</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<blockquote>
<div>
<p><strong>Scope creep</strong> is […​] the <em>incremental expansion</em> of the scope of a
project […​], while nevertheless <em>failing to adjust</em> the schedule and
budget.</p>
</div>
</blockquote>
<div>
— Wikipedia<br/>
<cite>Scope (project_management)</cite>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<h3 id="user-content-problem-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#problem-analysis" id="user-content-problem-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Problem Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>Problem analysis is critical. When we say, <em>problem</em>, we can also think
<em>goal</em>, <em>research question</em>, or <em>issue</em>. If you don’t get this right,
you can waste a lot of time and money solving the <em>wrong problem</em> or
address a <em>non-issue</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>We can summarize the key points of problem
analysis<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_9" id="user-content-_footnoteref_9" title="View footnote.">9</a>]</sup> as:</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Define and clarify the problem (or issue)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What exactly are we trying to solve?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Determine the problem’s importance</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>How much does it matter?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Assess the feasibility of solving the problem</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Do we have the resources we need?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Consider any negative impacts (unintended consequences)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What could go wrong?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Prioritize problems to solve (bottlenecks? low-hanging fruit?)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What are the most critical issues?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Answer: <em>what</em>, <em>why</em>, <em>who</em>, <em>when</em>, <em>where</em>, and <em>how much</em>?</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>Drill down into the problem with all kinds of questions to expose
dependencies.</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
<li>
<p>Find <strong>causes</strong> (especially
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Root_cause_analysis" rel="nofollow">root cause</a>) and
<strong>symptoms</strong> (effects)</p>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><em>What is really going on here?</em></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-root-cause-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#root-cause-analysis" id="user-content-root-cause-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Root Cause Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>There are several methods of root cause analysis, but one simple one is
"Ask Why Five Times" …</p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Root_Cause_Analysis_Tree_Diagram.jpg" rel="nofollow"><img alt="Root Cause Analysis Tree Diagram" height="359" src="images/root_cause_anal.jpg" width="430"/></a>
</div>
<div>Root Cause Analysis Tree Diagram - Image: KellyLawless, CC BY-SA 3.0 Unported</div>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p>… where you keep asking "Why? But, why? But, Why?" over and over
again until there is a clear root cause that you can actually do
something about.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>A real example is mentioned in
<a href="http://en.wikibooks.org/wiki/Business_Analysis_Guidebook/Root_Cause_Analysis#Ask_Why_5_Times" rel="nofollow">Ask
Why 5 Times, Business Analysis Guidebook/Root Cause Analysis
(wikibooks.org)</a>, where the US National Park Service was able to slow
the rate of deterioration of the Jefferson memorial simply by changing
the lighting schedule.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_10" id="user-content-_footnoteref_10" title="View footnote.">10</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>This method may also be used in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">Requirements Analysis</a>.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-requirements-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-analysis" id="user-content-requirements-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Analysis</h3>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Overview" rel="nofollow">Elicit,
Analyze, and Record (EAR)</a>:</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>System and project
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis" rel="nofollow">requirements</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>EAR Analysis is all about <em>listening</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Gather requirements by interviewing stakeholders. Observe how people
currently do their work or use the system. Make sure the requirements
are detailed, clear, unambigious, and comprehensive. Document the
requirements as a list, in diagrams, or
narratives.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_11" id="user-content-_footnoteref_11" title="View footnote.">11</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>As an example, in Spring 2014, we held a meeting with our graduate
students and asked them about what sort of computing needs they had. We
had a discussion, <em>listened</em> to what they had to say, summarized the
main concerns, and documented them.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Further elucidate
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Measurable_goals" rel="nofollow">Measurable
goals</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>By continuing to ask questions like "Why? …​ Why? …​ Why? …​", get
more specific until the goals become quantifiable. Measured goals are
easier to meet since you can measure your progress in achieving them.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Mission objectives determine the goals. Compare the stated goals against
mission objectives to produce a small set of critical, measured goals.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Output:
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_specification" rel="nofollow">Requirements
specification</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>This document will be used in the <em>logical design</em> phase to model the
relationships within the system. So, it should be clear and thorough.
The more specific and unambiguous this specification is, the easier it
will be to design the system to meet the requirements.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>One way to be more clear is to organize the requirements by type.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-requirements-categories">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-categories" id="user-content-requirements-categories"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Categories</h4>
<div>
<p>You may categorize requirements according to several important types:</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Requirements_analysis#Types_of_Requirements" rel="nofollow">Types
of requirements</a>:</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Operational requirements are the <strong>utility, effectiveness, and
deployment</strong> needs, with repect to practical use of the system within the
overall operation. These are the <strong>customer Requirements</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_requirement" rel="nofollow">Functional</a>
requirements are specific things the system must <strong>do</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-functional_requirement" rel="nofollow">Non-functional</a>
requirements are specific things the system must <strong>provide</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Systems_architecture" rel="nofollow">Architectural</a>
requirements define how the system must be <strong>structured</strong>, in other words,
how the components must <strong>interrelate</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Behavioral requirements describe how <strong>users</strong> and other systems will
<strong>interact</strong> with the system and how the <strong>system</strong> will respond.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Performance requirements define <strong>how well</strong> the systems needs to do
things, <strong>measured</strong> in terms of quantity, quality, coverage, timeliness
or readiness.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>While there are other ways to group requirements, these are the most
significant categories.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-requirements-modeling-example">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#requirements-modeling-example" id="user-content-requirements-modeling-example"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Requirements Modeling: Example</h3>
<div>
<p>Another way to make requirements clear is through <em>requirements
modeling</em>. Here is an example of modeling behavioral requirements with a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Use_case" rel="nofollow">Use Case Diagram</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p><strong>Survey Data System</strong></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The behavioral requirements are stated in <strong>role</strong> <em>goal</em> format.</p>
</div>
<div>
<ul>
<li>
<p><strong>Researcher</strong> <em>uploads survey</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Subject</strong> <em>takes survey</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Subject</strong> <em>uploads results</em>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Researcher</strong> <em>downloads results</em>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div>
<p>Gramatically speaking, the role is the <em>subject</em> and the goal is stated
as a <em>verb</em> and its <em>object</em>. Each of these requirements form the basis
of a <em>use case</em>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-use-case-diagram">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#use-case-diagram" id="user-content-use-case-diagram"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Use Case Diagram</h4>
<div>
<p>The <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Use_case" rel="nofollow">Use Case Diagram</a> is a
standard means of showing these requirements pictorially.</p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<a href="https://github.com/brianhigh/data-workshop/blob/master/images/research_survey_data_system.png"><img alt="Research Survey Data System" height="241" src="images/research_survey_data_system.png" width="414"/></a>
</div>
<div>Research Survey Data System</div>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p>Here, human <em>actors</em> are depicted as stick figures. These are the
<strong>roles</strong>. The <em>goal</em> is the line connecting the role to a system activity
or function, shown as an oval. In this example, the <em>system</em> is drawn as
a box with the actors outside the box. The reason is that the system’s
<em>product scope</em> was defined to be the electronic data system. The human
actors interact with that system. The information flowing to and from an
actor, as well as data flows within a system, will be modeled in the
<em>logical design</em>.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-logical-design">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#logical-design" id="user-content-logical-design"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Logical Design</h3>
<div>
<p>Once system behaviours have been modeled from the perspective of user
interaction, we can begin to model the internal workings of the system
with the <em>logical design</em>.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_12" id="user-content-_footnoteref_12" title="View footnote.">12</a>]</sup> The <em>logical</em> part means that this is an <em>abstract
representation</em> of the system. Therefore, the system is modeled in terms
of abstractions such as <em>data flows, entities, and relationships</em>. We
model how the system will satisfy functional requirements such as <em>inputs
and outputs</em>. To make the abstract more concrete, we make use of
<em>Graphical modeling</em> techniques to produce graphics such as the
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_flow_diagram" rel="nofollow">Data Flow Diagram (DFD)</a>
and
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity%E2%80%93relationship_model" rel="nofollow">Entity
Relationship Diagram (ERD)</a>.</p>
</div>
<div>
<h4 id="user-content-example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd" id="user-content-example-entity-relationship-diagram-erd"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Example Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD)</h4>
<div>
<p>For example, let’s consider a system which records the playlists of
musical performances at a local pub. You will want to store which artist
performs which song. The relationship between an artist and a song can
be shown in a simple
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity%E2%80%93relationship_model" rel="nofollow">Entity
Relationship Diagram</a>. We start with the behavioral <em>use case</em> written
as <strong>Artist</strong> <em>performs Song</em>. Then, using a standard set of symbols, we
can produce this diagram.</p>
</div>
<div>
<div>
<a href="http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:ERD-artist-performs-song.svg" rel="nofollow"><img alt="ERD: Artist performs song" height="82" src="images/320px-ERD-artist-performs-song.png" width="320"/></a>
</div>
<div>ERD: Artist performs song</div>
</div>
<hr/>
<div>
<p>The boxes represent <em>entities</em> and the line connecting them is the
relationship. Here, the symbols indicate that there is a one-to-many
relationship. This diagram is saying that exactly one artist performs
one or more songs, (or doesn’t perform any).</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Since this "system" describes only solo performers, you would want to
model it differently to include groups of artists that perform more than
one song. You would want a many-to-many relationship as well as other
entities to represent the musical groups. Since there will by many
performances, you will also want an entity to represent the
performances.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The value of this type of diagram is that complex relationships can be
mapped to a great level of detail. In fact, they can be used to
automatically generate database schemas.</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div>
<h3 id="user-content-logical-design-diagrams">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#logical-design-diagrams" id="user-content-logical-design-diagrams"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Logical Design: Diagrams</h3>

<a href="https://github.com/brianhigh/data-workshop/blob/master/Systems_Analysis_and_Design.md">tutorial</a><sup>[<a href="#_footnote_13" id="user-content-_footnoteref_13" title="View footnote.">13</a>]</sup>

<h3 id="user-content-decision-analysis">
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#decision-analysis" id="user-content-decision-analysis"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Decision Analysis</h3>
<div>
<p>These models and diagrams will help you make a very important decision.
Before you go further in the system design process, you need to decide
whether to buy a system or build one. Or you may consider building a
system of components, some of which you might buy and others might be
custom built. You will want to come up with a few of the best
alternatives and present them to your stakeholders. In your case that
might be your lab manager, principal investigator, or funding agency.</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>Your presentation will also include a
<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_analysis" rel="nofollow">Decision analysis</a> to
weigh the pros and cons of the various options against the requirements
in order to help the stakeholders with their decision. You should
conclude your analysis with a recommendation of your top choice and
explain why this choice is the most compelling. Then you will want to
get a decision, and the approval to continue, before you invest any more
time on further analysis.<sup>[<a href="#_footnote_14" id="user-content-_footnoteref_14" title="View footnote.">14</a>]</sup></p>
</div>
<div>
<p>The system development life cycle (SDLC) continues on to other phases,
which we do not have time to cover here. However, we hope that this
glimpse at the systems analysis phase has demonstrated the value of this
approach in gathering and clarifying requirements that can be used to
design and build an information system.</p>
</div>
