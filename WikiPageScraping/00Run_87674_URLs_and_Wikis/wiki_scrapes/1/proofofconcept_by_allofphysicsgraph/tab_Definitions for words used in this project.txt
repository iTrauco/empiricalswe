          <p>atom</p>
<ul>
<li>a statement which cannot be broken down into smaller statements.</li>
</ul>
<p>axiom</p>
<ul>
<li>a statement or proposition that is regarded as being established, accepted, or self-evidently true.</li>
</ul>
<p>complete</p>
<ul>
<li>for every statement of a language of the system either the statement or it's negation can be derived.</li>
</ul>
<p>consistent</p>
<ul>
<li>No statement and it's negation are both derivable</li>
</ul>
<p>postulate</p>
<ul>
<li>suggest or assume the existence, fact, or truth of (something) as a basis for reasoning, discussion, or belief</li>
</ul>
<p>derivation</p>
<ul>
<li>the obtaining or developing of something from a source or origin.</li>
</ul>
<p>function</p>
<ul>
<li>A mathematical relation such that each element of a given set (domain of the function) is associated with an   element of another set ( range of the function)</li>
</ul>
<p>inference rule</p>
<ul>
<li>a logical form consisting of a function which takes premises, analyzes their syntax, and returns a conclusion (or conclusions)</li>
<li>aka transformation rule</li>
</ul>
<p>lemma</p>
<ul>
<li>a subsidiary or intermediate theorem in an argument or proof.</li>
</ul>
<p>operator</p>
<ul>
<li>a symbol or function representing a mathematical operation</li>
</ul>
<p>statement</p>
<ul>
<li>aka "expression" in Mathematica</li>
<li>includes equations and inequalities</li>
<li>has LHS, RHS</li>
</ul>
<p>theorem</p>
<ul>
<li>a general proposition not self-evident but proved by a chain of reasoning; a truth established by means of accepted truths.</li>
</ul>
<p>terms</p>
<ul>
<li>A+B</li>
</ul>
<p>operators</p>
<ul>
<li>+,x</li>
</ul>
<p>products</p>
<ul>
<li>A*B</li>
</ul>
