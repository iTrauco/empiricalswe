<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#list-of-degrees" id="user-content-list-of-degrees"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>List of degrees</h1>
<p>The <strong>CENTRE &gt; Degrees</strong> option displays a list of all degrees in the selected centre. A degree is a career, diploma, degree, postgraduate, master, cycle or level. The list is sorted alphabetically by degree name.</p>
<p>Each of the rows of the list contains the following columns:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Degree</strong>: logo and name of the degree.</li>
<li>
<strong>Type</strong>: type of degree, eg undergraduate, graduate or master. System administrators can edit the types of degree by clicking on the <a href="CENTRE.DegreeTypes.en">Types of degree</a> icon in the upper left corner of the box.</li>
<li>
<strong>Courses</strong>: number of courses in the degree.</li>
<li>
<strong>Status</strong>: <em>New</em> (pending review) or <em>Active</em>.</li>
</ul>
<h1>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#editing-degrees" id="user-content-editing-degrees"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Editing degrees</h1>
<p>Any user can add a degree by clicking on the <em>Edit</em> icon in the upper left corner of the box or the <em>Create another degree</em> button at the end of the list.</p>
<p>First SWAD shows a form to <strong>create a new degree</strong>, with the following fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>Short name of the degree.</li>
<li>Full name of the degree.</li>
<li>Type: a selector with all types available. System administrators can edit the types of degree by clicking on the <a href="CENTRE.DegreeTypes.en">Types of degree</a> icon in the upper left corner of the box.</li>
<li>WWW: full URL, including <code>http://</code> or <code>https://</code>
</li>
</ul>
<p>When a user creates a new degree, the degree goes to status <em>Pending</em>, which means "pending review", although it is fully usable. System administrators can visit <a href="SYSTEM.Hierarchy.en#pending">SYSTEM &gt; Hierarchy &gt; Pending</a> to check for centres with degrees that are pending review.</p>
<p>Below that box it is offered a <strong>list with all current degrees</strong> in the selected centre where centre administrators, institution administrators and system administrators can edit names, types and WWW fields and eliminate those degrees that do not have any course and any user. A centre administrator, institution administrator or system administrator can also activate pending degrees after fixing errors and checking them. If the creator of the degree is not a centre administrator, institution administrator or system administrator, he/she can still continue editing the degree while it is in the <em>pending</em> status.</p>
