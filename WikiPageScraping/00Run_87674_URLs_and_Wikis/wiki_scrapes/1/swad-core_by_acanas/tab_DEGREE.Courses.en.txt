<h1>

<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#list-courses" id="user-content-list-courses"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>List courses</h2>
<p>The <strong>DEGREE &gt; Courses</strong> option displays a list of all courses in the selected degree. A course is a subject or discipline. The list is sorted first by year or semester and second by course name.</p>
<p>Each of the rows of the list contains the following columns:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Institutional code</strong>: alphanumeric code of course in the institution (optional).</li>
<li>
<strong>Year or semester</strong>: number from 1 to 12, or N.A. (not applicable) if you do not know the year or the course can be studied in several years/semesters.</li>
<li>
<strong>Course</strong>: name of the course.</li>
<li>
<strong>Teachers</strong>: number of teachers of the course.</li>
<li>
<strong>Students</strong>: number of students enrolled in the course.</li>
<li>
<strong>Status</strong>: <em>New</em> (pending review) or <em>Active</em>.</li>
</ul>
<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#edit-courses" id="user-content-edit-courses"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Edit courses</h2>
<p>You can create new courses and edit existing courses by clicking on the <em>Edit</em> icon (pencil) in the upper left corner of the list of courses (<strong>DEGREE &gt; Courses</strong>) or the <em>Create another course</em> button at the end of the list.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#create-a-new-course" id="user-content-create-a-new-course"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Create a new course</h3>
<p>SWAD shows a box with a form to <strong>create a new course</strong>, with the following fields:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Institutional code</strong>: alphanumeric code of course in the institution (optional).</li>
<li>
<strong>Year or semester</strong>: a selector with all years/semesters available.</li>
<li>
<strong>Short name</strong> of the course.</li>
<li>
<strong>Full name</strong> of the course.</li>
</ul>
<p>When a user creates a new course, the course goes to status <em>New</em>, which means "pending review", although it is fully usable. System administrators can visit <a href="SYSTEM.Hierarchy.en#pending">SYSTEM &gt; Hierarchy &gt; Pending</a> to check for degrees with courses that are pending review.</p>
<h3>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#edit-existing-courses" id="user-content-edit-existing-courses"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Edit existing courses</h3>
<p>Below the box to create a new course, a second box to <strong>edit courses</strong> is shown.</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>If you are logged as degree <strong>administrator</strong>, centre administrator, institution administrator or system administrator, you can:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>edit</strong> codes, years/semesters and names</li>
<li>
<strong>remove</strong> those courses that do not have any user by clicking on the <em>Remove</em> icon (red cross)</li>
<li>
<strong>activate</strong> pending courses after fixing errors and checking them</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p>If you are the <strong>creator</strong> of a course, and you are not logged as administrator, and the course is still in the <em>New</em> status, you can:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>edit</strong> code, year and names</li>
<li>
<strong>remove</strong> the course (if it do not have any user) by clicking on the <em>Remove</em> icon (red cross)</li>
</ul>
<p>As a safety measure, in order to eliminate a course it is imperative that no users belong to that course, that is, the course does not have students or teachers. If you are logged in as a teacher or administrator, you can remove users from the course in <a href="USERS.Students.en">USERS &gt; Students</a> and <a href="USERS.Teachers.en">USERS &gt; Teachers</a>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
