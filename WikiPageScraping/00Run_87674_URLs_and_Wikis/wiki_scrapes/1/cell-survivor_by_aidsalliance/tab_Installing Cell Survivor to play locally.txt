Installing Cell Survivor to play on a PC</h1>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Download the game by clicking <a href="https://github.com/aidsalliance/cell-survivor/archive/gh-pages.zip">this link.</a> It’s about 2Mb, so it may take a minute or two on a slow Internet connection.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>After the download has completed, locate the ZIP file ‘cell-survivor-gh-pages.zip’ which you have just downloaded.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Double-click the ZIP file to open it, and drag the ‘cell-survivor-gh-pages’ folder from the compressed folder to a new location. Drag the original ZIP file to the Recycle Bin, where you can delete it later.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Double-click the file ‘index.html’ in the top level of the uncompressed ‘cell-survivor-gh-pages’ folder. This should open a new window in your default web browser.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Play the game! You will be able to play Cell Survivor on your PC even when it’s not connected to the Internet.</p>
</li>
</ol>

Tinkering with Cell Survivor: Installing the development environment</h1>
<p>If you would like to learn how Cell Survivor works, or change the way it works, or learn how to create your own computer game, then follow the intructions below. You will need to install ‘Sublime Text 2’ (which is free to try), and some other development tools (all are free to use), so you will need administrator access to your computer. You will also need a good Internet connection to complete these steps, but after that you will be able to modify your copy of the Cell Survivor game without needing an Internet connection.</p>
<ol>
<li>Install Sublime Text 2</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>Visit the <a href="http://www.sublimetext.com/2" rel="nofollow">Sublime Text 2 downoad page</a> and follow the instructions to download ‘Sublime Text 2’ on your PC or Mac.</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>Install node and npm</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>Visit the <a href="http://nodejs.org/download/" rel="nofollow">Node.js download page</a> and follow the instructions to download Node on your PC or Mac.</p>
<ol start="3">
<li>Install Git</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>Visit the <a href="http://git-scm.com/downloads" rel="nofollow">Git download page</a> and follow the instructions to download Node on your PC or Mac.</p>
<ol start="4">
<li>Make a folder to contain Cell Survivor</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>Open you computer’s terminal. On a PC this is ‘Command Prompt’ in the ‘Accessories’ option of the ‘All Programs’ menu. On a Mac, this is ‘Terminal’ in the ‘Utilities’ folder inside the ‘Applications’ folder.</p>
<p>When the terminal is ready, you should see the 'prompt' character, which is usually a dollar sign followed by a blinking cursor. Type this text after the dollar sign, and hit the 'return' key:<br/>
<code>mkdir ~/cell-survivor</code><br/>
...which will create a new folder in your home folder. Now type:<br/>
<code>cd ~/cell-survivor</code></p>
<ol start="5">
<li>Download the Cell Survivor files from GitHub</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>This may take up to ten minutes if you have a slow Internet connection.<br/>
<code>$ git clone https://github.com/aidsalliance/cell-survivor.git</code><br/>
...after the download has completed, move into the repo folder be retyping:<br/>
<code>cd ~/cell-survivor</code></p>
<ol start="6">
<li>Install developer dependencies from NPM</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>This may take up to three hours if you have a slow Internet connection.<br/>
<code>$ npm install</code></p>
<ol start="7">
<li>Install Gulp</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>Visit <a href="https://github.com/gulpjs/gulp/blob/master/docs/getting-started.md">Gulp’s Getting Started guide</a> and follow the instructions to install Gulp.</p>
<ol start="8">
<li>Start tinkering!</li>
</ol>
<hr/>
<p>That’s it! You have everything you need to run a development environment for Cell Survivor.</p>
