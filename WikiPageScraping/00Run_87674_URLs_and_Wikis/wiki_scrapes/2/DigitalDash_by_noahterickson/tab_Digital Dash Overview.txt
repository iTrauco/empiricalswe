<p>The increased complexity of electrical systems on board a Formula SAE car requires the driver to monitor critical parameters while operating the vehicle. The digital dash device facilitates the process of letting the driver know what is happening with the car.</p>

<h2>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#how-does-it-work" id="user-content-how-does-it-work"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>How does it work?</h2>
<p>A Formula SAE electric car has battery management and motor controller systems that send out messages on CAN buses. The digital dash connects to the vehicles CAN bus and searches for specific messages, translates them and displays them onto a screen for the driver to see. There are a few key components to the digital dash that can be further reviewed in the <a href="https://github.com/noahterickson/digitaldash/wiki/building-overview">Building Overview</a> section.</p>
