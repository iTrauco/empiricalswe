<h4>
<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#hung-console-on-exit" id="user-content-hung-console-on-exit"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a>Hung console on exit</h4>
<p>When exiting console, sometimes it will give a message like <code>Terminating 2 actors</code> and just hang. Bypassing this problem is a simple 2 step process:
First, press <code>Ctrl+z</code>. You should now see the following:</p>
<pre><code>^Z
[1]+  Stopped                 bundle exec irb -r gemname
</code></pre>
<p>The 1 in square brackets is the process number. The command below may need to be edited to reflect a different process number. If we send it the
TERMinate message twice using the <code>kill</code> utility, it will cleanly shut down the process:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-shell"><pre><span class="pl-c1">kill</span> -TERM %1<span class="pl-k">;</span> <span class="pl-c1">kill</span> -TERM %1</pre></div>
<p>Wait a moment and just press enter again, and you should see:</p>
<pre><code>[1]+  Exit 143                bundle exec irb -r gemname
</code></pre>
