<p>Here you will find a list of <strong>open source</strong> generator plugins. If there is no source, the plugin will not be listed. Github is preferred for code hosting. Do not put JAR downloads here.</p>
<p>You may add your plugin using the BukkitFullOfMoon template, but be warned, if you don't post the source, your entry will be deleted.</p>
<p>These are listed in <em>alphabetical</em> order.</p>

<a aria-hidden="true" class="anchor" href="#how-to-use-generator-plugins-with-multiverse" id="user-content-how-to-use-generator-plugins-with-multiverse"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z" fill-rule="evenodd"></path></svg></a><a href="#wiki-howto" name="user-content-howto">•</a>How to use generator plugins with Multiverse</h2>
<ol>
<li>Place the generator's jar in your plugins folder.</li>
<li>Start your server</li>
<li>Use the generator command. This will verify that the generator loaded properly. You should see its name appear:</li>
</ol>
<pre><code>/mv gens
--- Loaded Generator Plugins ---
CleanroomGenerator SkylandsPlus 
</code></pre>
<ol start="4">
<li>Then simply use the create command to create the world with a generator.</li>
</ol>
<pre><code>/mv create skyworld2 normal -g CleanroomGenerator
Starting creation of world 'skyworld2'...
Complete!
</code></pre>
