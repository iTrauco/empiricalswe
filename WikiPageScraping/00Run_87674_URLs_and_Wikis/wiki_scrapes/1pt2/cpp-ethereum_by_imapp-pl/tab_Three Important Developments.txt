<p>The oft-tauted "memory hardness" attribute of a proof-of-work (PoW) algorithm is a perhaps sufficient, but not required, attribute in order to guarantee a more-or-less linear investment:payback curve (super-linear curves, where a large investment yields crazy-high returns, as delivered by the first ASICs, are problematic since they concentrate power away from everybody and into the hands of only a few key ASIC investors).</p>
<p>Another way of reducing the "ASIC issue", is to make the algorithm so similar to that of a general-purpose processing unit (a CPU) that implementing as an ASIC becomes pointless.</p>
