<p>Qs. How should one go about learning the Go language? What material (books, eBooks, online tutorials etc.) would you recommend?</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Andrew: First stop is the <a href="http://tour.golang.org/" rel="nofollow">Go Tour</a>. Then get Go installed and <strong>you must read</strong> the “<a href="http://golang.org/doc/code.html" rel="nofollow">How to Write Go Code</a>” page. Then, check out the articles linked from <a href="http://golang.org/doc/" rel="nofollow">golang.org/doc/</a> and the <a href="http://blog.golang.org/" rel="nofollow">Go Blog</a> in general. I like <a href="https://gobyexample.com/" rel="nofollow">Go By Example</a> for lots of clean examples of Go in action. There’s also the very nice <a href="http://learnxinyminutes.com/docs/go/" rel="nofollow">Learn Go in Y minutes</a> document, which walks through most of Go’s language features.</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>If you’re the kind of person who would prefer to kick back and watch some videos, try my “<a href="http://talks.golang.org/2012/chat.slide#1" rel="nofollow">Code that grows with grace</a>” and “<a href="http://talks.golang.org/2012/simple.slide#1" rel="nofollow">A simple programming environment</a>”, as well as Russ Cox’s “<a href="http://youtu.be/ytEkHepK08c" rel="nofollow">A Tour of Go</a>”.</p>
</blockquote>
